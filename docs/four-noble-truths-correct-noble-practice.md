# Four Noble Truths As Correct Noble Practice

This note governs one practical OSF-facing explanation that the repository now
allows and preserves:

- wake up and look at what is here
- see where it is heading
- make a wholesome change
- notice and appreciate the quenching of dukkha
- do it again when remembered

This is not a replacement for the Four Noble Truths.
It is a controlled explanation of how the Four Noble Truths and their turnings
can function as lived practice.

## Governance

- Keep the doctrinal labels stable:
  `dukkha`, `samudaya`, `nirodha`, and `magga` remain governed by their own
  entries and are not replaced by slogan-language.
- Allow the practical cycle in notes and practice-facing explanation where it
  improves translator control.
- Do not let this explanation flatten the distinct tasks:
  seeing dukkha is not yet the same as quenching it,
  wholesome change is not a substitute for the whole path,
  and congratulating oneself is allowed only when the line refers to an actual
  quenching of dukkha rather than motivational self-talk detached from practice.

## Practical Mapping

- `dukkha`: wake up and look at what is here.
- `samudaya`: see where the present pattern is heading.
- `magga`: make a wholesome change.
- `nirodha`: notice the quenching of dukkha.
- `sati`: remember to do this again.

## Drift Guards

- Do not turn the Four Noble Truths into a merely therapeutic slogan.
- Do not turn `magga` into generic self-improvement.
- Do not turn `nirodha` into vague positivity or congratulation rhetoric.
- Do not turn `sati` into bare attention.
- Do not let this practice explanation erase the already stabilized distinctions
  among `kusala`, `virāga`, `nirodha`, `vimutti`, and `nibbāna`.

# Jhana Cluster Map

This note governs the repository's handling of the core `jhāna / samādhi`
family.
It is not a general essay. It exists to stop jhāna-side language from
collapsing into one generic blur of `meditation`, `concentration`, or
`trance`.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Functional Role | Do Not Collapse Into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `jhāna` | `mental theme` | Governing jhāna-side headword in the fourfold sequence | meditation, absorption, trance, ecstasy |
| `jhāyati` | `think like this` | Verb-side practical instruction linked to the family | meditate, enter absorption |
| `samādhi` | `mental composure` | Broader composure-side headword | concentration, absorption, unification of mind |
| `sammā-samādhi` | `right mental composure` | Path-factor form governed as the fourfold jhāna sequence | right concentration |
| `vitakka` | `thinking` | First-jhāna active thought-side factor | applied thought, initial thought |
| `vicāra` | `pondering` | Ongoing consideration-side factor | sustained thought |
| `ekaggatā` | `directness` | Single-aim or unified-direction factor inside the family | one-pointedness, concentration |
| `pīti` | `delight` | Uplifted or energizing factor in the sequence | ecstasy |
| `sukha` | `satisfaction` | Pleasant settled tone within the family | happiness |
| `nīvaraṇa` | `distraction` | Obstructing family that must be kept distinct from jhāna language | enemy, problem |
| `bhāvanā` | `development` | Practice-development term, not a synonym for jhāna itself | meditation |

## Operational Reading Order

1. If the text names `jhāna` directly, keep `mental theme` as the family default.
2. If the text names `sammā-samādhi`, keep explicit that the path factor is the
   first, second, third, and fourth `jhāna`.
3. If the text is discussing the factors inside the sequence, let `thinking`,
   `pondering`, `directness`, `delight`, and `satisfaction` remain distinct.
4. If the text is discussing what blocks entry or settling, use the
   `nīvaraṇa` family rather than flattening the cluster into generic
   meditation-language.

## Family Logic

- `jhāna` governs the fourfold sequence as `first mental theme`,
  `second mental theme`, `third mental theme`, and `fourth mental theme`.
- `samādhi` remains the broader composure headword that interfaces with the
  jhāna family without collapsing into concentration-language.
- `sammā-samādhi` belongs to the path-factor family and the jhāna family at the
  same time. Keep both sides audible.
- `vitakka`, `vicāra`, `ekaggatā`, `pīti`, and `sukha` are role terms inside
  the family, not interchangeable synonyms for the whole state.
- `bhāvanā` is practice development. Do not make it a loose substitute for
  `jhāna`.

## Translator Guidance

- Do not translate `jhāna` as `absorption`, `trance`, or `ecstasy` by default.
- Do not let `samādhi` drift back to uncontrolled `concentration` language.
- Do not rewrite `ekaggatā` as `one-pointedness` when `directness` is the live
  house default.
- Keep `pīti` and `sukha` distinct: uplifted delight is not the same thing as
  settled satisfaction.
- Keep `nīvaraṇa` as the obstruction-side family rather than a mood label or
  spiritual enemy label.

## Interface Guardrails

- Path-factor interface:
  `sammā-samādhi` is the path-side form of the jhāna family and should remain
  coordinated with `sammā-diṭṭhi`, `sammā-sati`, and `sammā-vāyāma`.
- Practice interface:
  `bhāvanā` names development or cultivation; it does not erase the sharper
  jhāna-side vocabulary.
- Hindrance interface:
  `nīvaraṇa` remains the active obstruction family; do not replace it with
  generic jhāna-failure language.

## Main Drift Risks

- `jhāna` -> meditation, absorption, trance
- `samādhi` -> concentration
- `sammā-samādhi` -> right concentration
- `ekaggatā` -> one-pointedness
- `pīti` -> ecstasy
- `bhāvanā` -> meditation

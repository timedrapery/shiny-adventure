# MN 99 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 99: Subha Sutta](mn99-subha-sutta.md). The main translation is the
primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 99 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `gahaṭṭha`, `pabbajita`, `nīvaraṇa`, `kāmacchanda`, `byāpāda`,
  `thīna-middha`, `uddhacca-kukkucca`, `vicikicchā`, `jhāna`, `mettā`,
  `karuṇā`, `muditā`, `upekkhā`, `brahmavihāra`, and `cāga`.
- Working method: the brahmin fivefold list, the five-distractions section,
  the delight-and-jhāna contrast, and the four divine-abiding lines are
  checked as coordinated blocks so the discourse reads as one practical
  argument rather than as disconnected inherited formulas.

## Governing Decisions

- `gahaṭṭha` remains `householder` and `pabbajita` remains `renunciate`.
- The five `nīvaraṇa` members stay inside the repository's
  distraction-language: `sensual distraction`, `aversive distraction`,
  `dull distraction`, `agitated distraction`, and `uncertain distraction`.
- The delight contrast stays aligned with current `jhāna` policy:
  `first mental theme` and `second mental theme`, with `thinking and
  pondering`, `inner clarity`, and `unification of mind`.
- The brahmavihāra close uses current house terms:
  `friendliness`, `kindness towards others`, `gladness`, and
  `dynamic balance`.
- `mettā cetovimutti`, `karuṇā cetovimutti`, `muditā cetovimutti`, and
  `upekkhā cetovimutti` are rendered as `liberation of mind through ...`
  expressions rather than as vague mood language.

## Re-audit Calls

- `vibhajjavādo kho ahamettha` is rendered `On this point, student, I
  analyze` so the Buddha's reply keeps its discriminating, non-one-sided
  force without sounding scholastic.
- The farming and trade comparison is kept plain and practical. The point is
  not to rank busy life above renunciate life by default, but to say that both
  can fail and both can bear fruit depending on how they are carried through.
- The line-of-blind-men image and the blind-from-birth simile are both kept
  explicit. MN 99 presses the argument through visible blindness rather than
  abstract epistemology.
- `pañca kāmaguṇā` is rendered here as `five kinds of sensual stimulation`.
  That keeps the section readable while preserving the practical point that
  Pokkharasāti remains caught by agreeable sensual pull.
- The brahmin fivefold list is rendered `truth`, `austerity`,
  `chaste conduct`, `recitation`, and `generosity`. The localized
  `chaste conduct` choice is deliberate here: in this argument the brahmin
  list names specific merit practices, not the repository's broader Buddhist
  handling of `brahmacariya` as the whole spiritual life.
- `cittaṁ averaṁ abyābajjhaṁ tassa bhāvanāya` is rendered `for developing a
  mind free of enmity and ill will`. The line is kept practical and
  cultivation-facing rather than generalized into vague benevolence.
- `brahmānaṁ sahabyatāya maggo` is rendered `path to company with divinity`.
  That keeps the passage close to the brahmā-world destination being discussed
  without flattening the close into a merely symbolic mood statement.

## Practice Clarifications

- This discourse does not simply flatter householders or renunciates. It asks
  what actually succeeds and what actually fails.
- The Buddha does not accept the brahmin fivefold list as final in itself. He
  reclassifies those five as supports for cultivating a mind free of enmity and
  ill will.
- The delight contrast matters. Sensual delight is not treated as equal to the
  delight born from seclusion and mental composure.
- The brahmavihāra close is practical training language. It is not only a
  cosmological answer about Brahmā-world rebirth; it is a governed
  cultivation-path answer.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can
  function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- This companion file should preserve rationale for contested renderings,
  list-level decisions, and future edition guidance rather than repeat the
  main file line by line.

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first study edition of the repo's
  MN 99 translation surface.
- Future revisions should be driven by stronger source-audit gains, added term
  records for the brahmin fivefold list, or clear readability improvements,
  not routine word swapping.

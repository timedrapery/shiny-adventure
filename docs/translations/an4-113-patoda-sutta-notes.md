# AN 4.113 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[AN 4.113: Patoda Sutta](an4-113-patoda-sutta.md). The main translation is the
primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of AN 4.113 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially `samvega`,
  `yoniso`, `panna`, `kaya`, and `vedana`.
- Working method: the discourse was governed as the first Aṅguttara Nikāya
  translation surface in the repository, building on the urgency-and-striving
  formula that pairs `saṁvijjati / saṁvegaṁ āpajjati` with `yoniso padahati`.

## Governing Decisions

- `saṁvijjati saṁvegaṁ āpajjati` is rendered `is stirred and comes to
  urgency`. The headword record for `saṃvega` (minor, reviewed) gives
  `urgency` as the house default. The paired verb `saṁvijjati` is rendered
  `is stirred` to preserve the two-step movement — the initial shaking, then
  the arrival at urgency — rather than collapsing both into a single emotion
  word like alarm or dread.
- `saṁviggo yoniso padahati` is rendered `Gripped by urgency, they strive
  thoroughly`. The house term for `yoniso` is `thoroughly`; `padahati` is
  rendered `strive` as a clear, modern word for directed effort.
- `pahitatto kāyena ceva paramasaccaṁ sacchikaroti, paññāya ca ativijjha
  passati` is rendered `Applying themselves, they witness the ultimate truth
  with the body and see through it with discernment`. The house term for
  `kāya` is `body`; the house term for `paññā` is `discernment`. `Kāyena` is
  kept visible as `with the body` rather than absorbed into `personally` or
  `directly`, because this is the formulaic witness-contact with the deathless
  that precedes or accompanies penetrating seeing. `Sacchikaroti` is rendered
  `witness` to keep the direct-contact sense without importing later attainment
  jargon.
- `sārīrikāhi vedanāhi` in the fourth type is rendered `bodily felt
  experiences`. The house term for `vedanā` is `felt experience`; the
  qualifier `sārīrika` (bodily) is prefixed directly.

## Existing Control Records Reused

AN 4.113 does not need new live phrase records. The governing terms already
have sufficient lexicon coverage:

- `saṃvega` → `urgency` (minor, reviewed)
- `yoniso` → `thoroughly` (minor, reviewed)
- `paññā` → `discernment` (major, stable)
- `kāya` → `body` (major, stable)
- `vedanā` → `felt experience` (major, stable)

## Re-audit Calls

- `bhadra assājānīya` and `bhadra purisājānīya` are rendered `fine
  thoroughbred` horse and `fine thoroughbred individual`. No lexicon control
  governs these compounds. `Bhadra` (fine, noble, auspicious) + `ājānīya`
  (of good breed, knowing one's range) is a technical horse-breeding term
  repurposed as a spiritual analogy. `Fine thoroughbred` matches the standard
  English rendering and keeps the analogy readable without importing inflated
  nobility language.
- `patoda` is rendered `goad` — the spiked directing stick used by a horse
  trainer. This is the sutta's title term and the central image; `goad` is
  well-established and clear without requiring explanation.
- `patodacchāyā` is rendered `shadow of the goad`. The compound `cchāyā`
  (shadow) keeps the point: the most sensitive horse responds to the mere
  shadow before being touched at all.
- `lomavedhaviddha`, `cammavedhaviddha`, and `aṭṭhivedhaviddha` are rendered
  `struck through the hairs`, `struck through the hide`, and `struck to the
  bone`. These three-level penetration terms mark the decreasing sensitivity of
  the horses and, by analogy, the increasing distance from external stimulus
  needed to trigger urgency in the corresponding individual.
- `paramasaccaṁ` is rendered `ultimate truth`. The compound is transparent:
  `parama` (ultimate, highest) + `sacca` (truth). No lexicon record governs
  it; `ultimate truth` is used to avoid importing the later Madhyamaka
  two-truths vocabulary into a plain AN context.
- `ativijjha passati` is rendered `see through it with discernment`. The
  participle `ativijjha` (having penetrated through) + `passati` (sees)
  describes the penetrating knowing that follows direct witness. `See through
  it` captures the piercing sense without adding metaphysical commentary.
- The sutta has no opening nidāna in the Bilara segmentation; it begins
  directly at the teaching. The translation follows the source without adding a
  location marker.
- `Tatiyaṁ` (the chapter-internal counter at the end of the Pali) is rendered
  `The third sutta.` as a minimal identification marker.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation is divided into two sections: `The Four Thoroughbred
  Horses` and `The Four Thoroughbred Individuals`. These headings are editorial
  aids to keep the parallel structure readable across eight repeated units.
- The horse section serves as structural scaffolding for the person section;
  the comparison formula in each person-type passage connects both halves
  explicitly.
- Repetition across the four types is preserved rather than collapsed, because
  the cumulative weight of the progression — from distant news to personal
  affliction — is the sutta's teaching argument.

## Edition Status

- This companion supports the first stable study edition of the repo's AN 4.113
  translation surface, and the first Aṅguttara Nikāya surface in the
  repository.
- Future revisions should focus on the `saṁvijjati / saṁvega` rendering if
  later OSF practice material provides a stronger house default, or the
  `kāyena sacchikaroti` formula if the witness-contact rendering is revisited
  across the surface library.

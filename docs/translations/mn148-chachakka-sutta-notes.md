# MN 148 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 148: Chachakka Sutta](mn148-chachakka-sutta.md). The main translation is
the primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 148 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `āyatana`, `saḷāyatana`, `dhamma`, `viññāṇa`, `phassa`, `vedanā`, `taṇhā`,
  `sakkāya`, `attā`, `anattā`, `anusaya`, `pajānāti`, `nibbidā`, `virāga`,
  and `vimutti`.
- Working method: the sixfold list, the not-self analysis, the identity
  formulas, and the pleasant / painful / mixed feeling sequences are governed
  together so the discourse reads as one practical pattern rather than as a
  loose doctrinal catalog.

## Governing Decisions

- `Chachakka` is rendered `Six Sets of Six` because the discourse is organized
  around six linked sixfold sets, not around a vague slogan such as `six by
  six`.
- `āyatana` remains `field`, and the internal / external compounds are kept as
  `sense field` in the explicit sixfold inventory.
- `viññāṇa` remains `knowing`, including `eye-knowing`, `ear-knowing`, and
  `mind-knowing`.
- `phassa` remains `contact`.
- `vedanā` remains `felt experience` in the main contact chain, but the
  explicit three-register lines use `pleasant feeling`, `painful feeling`, and
  `mixed feeling`.
- `mixed` is deliberate. This edition does not use `neutral feeling` in MN
  148.
- `taṇhā` remains `ignorant wanting`, including in the sixfold list and the
  not-self / identity sections.
- `sakkāya` remains `identity`, so `sakkāyasamudayagāminī paṭipadā` becomes
  `the way leading to the arising of identity` and
  `sakkāyanirodhagāminī paṭipadā` becomes `the way leading to the quenching of
  identity`.
- `dhamma` remains untranslated in the mind-side object field because English
  equivalents narrow too quickly here.
- The positive and negative three-part appropriation formulas are now governed
  by dedicated records rather than left as document-only decisions.

## Re-audit Calls

- `veditabbāni` is kept source-facing as `are to be known`, not flattened into
  generic understand-language.
- The `-kāya` language in the sixfold list is rendered as `classes` so the
  grouping force stays visible without importing bodily `body` language into a
  technical inventory.
- `cakkhuñca paṭicca rūpe ca uppajjati cakkhuviññāṇaṁ` is rendered
  `dependent on eye and visible forms, eye-knowing arises` to keep both the
  conditioned structure and the visual-object force of `rūpa`.
- `abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati` is rendered
  `delights in it, affirms it, and keeps taking it personally`. That keeps the
  line aligned with the MN 18 `delight / affirm / take personally` family
  rather than turning it into generic approval or clinging language.
- The inverse pleasant- and painful-feeling response lines are now governed by
  dedicated records rather than remaining translation-local negations of the
  positive formulas.
- `tassā vedanāya samudayañca atthaṅgamañca assādañca ādīnavañca nissaraṇañca
  yathābhūtaṁ pajānāti` is now governed by a dedicated MN 148 phrase record
  rather than remaining translation-local.
- The mixed-feeling non-discernment line is now governed alongside the
  discernment line so the trained / untrained contrast stays explicit.
- `netaṁ mama, nesohamasmi, na me so attā` is not treated here as a bare
  metaphysical formula. It is a practical de-appropriation pattern.
- The middle four sense doors are compacted once the eye pattern is fully laid
  out and before the mind pattern is restated in full.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat MN 148 as a cold doctrinal taxonomy. It is a
  practical discourse about how the sixfold sense process becomes contact,
  felt experience, ignorant wanting, identity-taking, and either continued
  dissatisfaction or release.
- The not-self section matters because it spreads the analysis across the
  whole live chain: field, object-side field, knowing, contact, felt
  experience, and ignorant wanting.
- The identity section matters because the discourse does not leave
  appropriation abstract. It shows identity arising as taking each part of the
  process as mine, I am this, this is my self.
- The mixed-feeling line is structurally crucial. The point is not passive
  neutrality. The point is that ignorance remains when that register is not
  discerned through its arising, vanishing, gratification, danger, and escape.
- The final sequence from disenchantment to fading to release is therefore the
  practical outcome of understanding the sixfold process correctly, not an
  unrelated closing flourish.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can
  function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- Repeated sense-door material is partly compacted for readability once the
  governing pattern has been established.
- This companion file should preserve rationale for contested renderings,
  source-audit judgments, and future edition decisions rather than restating
  the entire main text.

## Re-audit Calls

- The opening object sequence is not treated as a metaphysical system to be
  decoded before the discourse can be read. The governing point is the
  repeated practical movement from recognition into selfing and delight.
- `sa챰j훮n훮ti` and `abhij훮n훮ti` are kept distinct in English as `recognizes`
  and `directly knows`, and that distinction is now stabilized in `terms/`.
- The `ma챰챰ati` lines are not rendered with abstract `conceives` language in
  the main file. This edition keeps the practical self-taking movement more
  explicit because that is what makes the sequence readable as training rather
  than as ontology, and that policy is now stabilized in the `ma챰챰ati`
  headword and the new MN 1 phrase records.
- `nibb훮na` is left untranslated even inside the list. That is deliberate. MN 1
  matters in part because it shows that even nibb훮na-language can be taken up

  trainee.
- Obscure cosmological names such as `Abhassara`, `Subhaki昭뇀a`, `Vehapphala`,
  and `Abhibh큰` are left close to the Pali rather than being over-confidently

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat MN 1 as a discourse about cataloging worlds. It
  is handled as a discourse about how appropriation spreads across whatever is
  taken up.
- The ordinary person does not merely have wrong theories. The discourse shows
  a live movement of recognition, selfing, possession, and delight.
- The trainee section is crucial because the wording stays prohibitive: the
  trainee still has work to do and should not keep feeding that movement.
- The arahant sections show the same field without appropriation or delight.
  The repeated secondary reasons about lust, aversion, and delusion are kept
  explicit because they prevent the discourse from becoming a merely
  intellectual correction.
- The last line matters. The bhikkhus do not delight in the Buddha's words.
  This edition preserves that ending plainly rather than softening it away.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can function
  as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- The ordinary-person and trainee movements are laid out in full because they
  establish the exact governing pattern.
- The later formally parallel movements are compacted once the English pattern
  is fully clear, so the study text stays readable without pretending that the
  repeated logic has changed.

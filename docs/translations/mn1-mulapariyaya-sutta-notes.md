# MN 1 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 1: Mūlapariyāya Sutta](mn1-mulapariyaya-sutta.md). The main translation is
the primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 1 in the SuttaCentral / Bilara
  tradition.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `puthujjana`, `sekha`, `arahant`, `tathāgata`, `nibbāna`, `sañjānāti`,
  `jānāti`, `pajānāti`, `yathābhūta-ñāṇadassana`, `pariññā`, `aññā`,
  `asmimāna`, and `yogakkhema`.
- Working method: the discourse is governed as one repeated structural pattern,
  not as a loose list of disconnected cosmological references.

## Governing Decisions

- `Mūlapariyāya` is rendered `The Root Sequence` in the title because the
  discourse works by a repeated root-pattern of recognition, selfing,
  ownership, and delight.
- `sañjānāti` remains `recognizes`.
- In this discourse, the `maññati` family is rendered with explicit
  selfing-language: `takes oneself to be`, `takes oneself in`, `takes oneself
  apart from`, and `takes ... as 'mine'`.
- `sekha` remains `trainee`.
- `pariññā` family language remains governed by `full understanding`.
- `nibbāna` remains untranslated.
- `Tathāgata`, `arahant`, and `buddha` remain untranslated as technical
  titles.

## Re-audit Calls

- The opening object sequence is not treated as a metaphysical system to be
  decoded before the discourse can be read. The governing point is the
  repeated practical movement from recognition into selfing and delight.
- `sañjānāti` and `abhijānāti` are kept distinct in English as `recognizes`
  and `directly knows`. The latter remains a document-level decision here
  pending a fuller verbal-family expansion in `terms/`.
- The `maññati` lines are not rendered with abstract `conceives` language in
  the main file. This edition keeps the practical self-taking movement more
  explicit because that is what makes the sequence readable as training rather
  than as ontology.
- `nibbāna` is left untranslated even inside the list. That is deliberate. MN 1
  matters in part because it shows that even nibbāna-language can be taken up
  wrongly by the ordinary person and still remains a field for training in the
  trainee.
- Obscure cosmological names such as `Abhassara`, `Subhakiṇha`, `Vehapphala`,
  and `Abhibhū` are left close to the Pali rather than being over-confidently
  rephrased into unstable English labels.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat MN 1 as a discourse about cataloging worlds. It
  is handled as a discourse about how appropriation spreads across whatever is
  taken up.
- The ordinary person does not merely have wrong theories. The discourse shows
  a live movement of recognition, selfing, possession, and delight.
- The trainee section is crucial because the wording stays prohibitive: the
  trainee still has work to do and should not keep feeding that movement.
- The arahant sections show the same field without appropriation or delight.
  The repeated secondary reasons about lust, aversion, and delusion are kept
  explicit because they prevent the discourse from becoming a merely
  intellectual correction.
- The last line matters. The bhikkhus do not delight in the Buddha's words.
  This edition preserves that ending plainly rather than softening it away.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can function
  as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- The ordinary-person and trainee movements are laid out in full because they
  establish the exact governing pattern.
- The later formally parallel movements are compacted once the English pattern
  is fully clear, so the study text stays readable without pretending that the
  repeated logic has changed.

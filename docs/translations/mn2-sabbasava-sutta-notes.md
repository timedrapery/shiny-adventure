# MN 2 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 2: Sabbāsava Sutta](mn2-sabbasava-sutta.md). The main translation is the
primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 2 as segmented in SuttaCentral's Bilara
  data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially `asava`,
  `asavanam-khaya`, `asavakkhaya`, `manasikara`, `yoniso-manasikara`,
  `ayoniso-manasikara`, `pahana`, `samvara`, `patisevana`, `adhivasana`,
  `parivajjana`, `vinodana`, `bhavana`, `dassana`, `puthujjana`,
  `ariyasavaka`, `ditthi`, `sakkaya-ditthi`, `vicikiccha`,
  `silabbata-paramasa`, `kamasava`, `bhavasava`, `avijjasava`,
  `sati-sambojjhanga`, `dhammavicaya`, `viriya`, `piti`, `passaddhi`,
  `samadhi`, `upekkha`, `viveka`, `viraga`, `nirodha`, `vossagga`,
  `tanha`, `samyojana`, `mana`, and `dukkha`.
- Working method: the opening attention contrast and the seven method headings
  are governed together so the sutta reads as a practical outflow-control
  sequence rather than as a loose list of unrelated remedies.

## Governing Decisions

- `Sabbāsava` is rendered `All the Outflows`.
- `āsava` remains `outflow`; the named compounds stay `sensual outflow`,
  `outflow of becoming`, and `outflow of ignorance`.
- `āsavānaṃ khayā` is represented in the opening as `the wearing away of the
  outflows`, keeping the standard release formula aligned with `khaya` and
  `asava`.
- `yoniso manasikāra` remains `wise attention`; `ayoniso manasikāra` remains
  `unwise attention`.
- The seven method labels stay explicit: `seeing`, `restraint`, `using`,
  `enduring`, `avoiding`, `dispelling`, and `developing`.
- `paṭisevanā` is rendered `using`, not `consuming`, because the requisites
  passage is about disciplined use, not indulgence or ownership.
- `adhivāsanā` is rendered `enduring`, but the notes keep it distinct from
  passivity or suppression.
- `parivajjanā` is rendered `avoiding`, with the situational force of steering
  clear rather than fearfulness.
- `vinodanā` is rendered `dispelling` so the active clearing-away sequence does
  not collapse into the broader `abandoning` frame.
- The awakening factors are rendered with the repository's established
  defaults: remembering, discernment of qualities, energy, delight, relaxation,
  mental composure, and dynamic balance.

## Re-audit Calls

- The opening title phrase `sabbāsavasaṃvarapariyāya` is handled as `the method
  for restraining all outflows` because the discourse is not only naming the
  outflows; it lays out a controlled sequence for dealing with them.
- The first method, seeing, is the longest section and should stay visibly
  different from the remaining six methods. It diagnoses unwise attention, the
  identity-producing question sequence, and the arising of view fetters.
- The six speculative questions are kept as questions rather than converted
  into abstract doctrine. Their function is to show unwise attention turning
  into self-positioning.
- The six views are rendered plainly. The phrase `saccato thetato` is handled
  as `as true and established` so the fixating force remains visible without
  adding scholastic language.
- The view-thicket sequence remains imagistic and cumulative: view-position,
  view-thicket, view-wilderness, view-distortion, view-wriggling, and fetter of
  views.
- The four-truth attention line stays aligned with the existing truth surfaces:
  dissatisfaction, origin, quenching, and path of practice leading to quenching.
- The three fetters use existing governed labels: identity view, uncertain
  distraction, and grasping at rules and observances.
- The sense-faculty restraint passage keeps the repeated distress-and-fever
  logic rather than paraphrasing it away.
- The requisites passage uses `felt experience` where the text names `vedanā`,
  keeping it aligned with the repo's broader felt-experience policy.
- The final line keeps `ignorant wanting`, `fetter`, `conceit`, and
  `dissatisfaction` audible so the close remains coordinated with the live
  `tanha`, `samyojana`, `mana`, and `dukkha` policies.

## Practice Clarifications

- MN 2 does not present one method for every situation. It differentiates what
  is abandoned by seeing, restraint, using, enduring, avoiding, dispelling, and
  developing.
- The opening contrast makes attention operative: outflows are fed or abandoned
  according to what is attended to and how it is attended to.
- The requisites section is part of the outflow sequence, not an independent
  ascetic austerity passage. Robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicines are used
  for practical support and restraint.
- The enduring section does not praise numbness. It describes bearing painful
  contact without letting reactive outflows multiply.
- The avoiding section is not timidity. It is intelligent steering clear of
  dangerous places, unsuitable contexts, and bad companionship.
- The dispelling section keeps active clearing-away language for unwholesome
  thoughts as they arise.
- The development section places the awakening factors inside the same outflow
  method sequence as the more immediate practical remedies.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the seven methods remain
  scannable as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in the
  root text beyond the source's method markers.
- Repeated formula material is compressed only where the Pali itself uses
  ellipsis; the seven method frame remains fully visible.
- The document keeps `bhikkhu` untranslated where the root address and practice
  context are monastic.

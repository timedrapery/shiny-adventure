## Translation

### The Setting

So I heard.

At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī.

There the Buddha addressed the bhikkhus: "Bhikkhus."

"Bhante," they replied.

The Buddha said this:

### The Dependent-Arising Sequence

"Bhikkhus, I will teach and analyze dependent arising for you. Listen
carefully, attend well, and I will speak."

"Yes, bhante," the bhikkhus replied.

The Buddha said this:

"And what, bhikkhus, is dependent arising?

With ignorance as condition, putting together;
with putting together as condition, knowing;
with knowing as condition, name-and-form;
with name-and-form as condition, the six fields of experience;
with the six fields of experience as condition, contact;
with contact as condition, felt experience;
with felt experience as condition, ignorant wanting;
with ignorant wanting as condition, taking personally;
with taking personally as condition, becoming;
with becoming as condition, birth;
with birth as condition, ageing and dying, sorrow, lamentation,
dissatisfaction, distress, and despair come to be.

That is how this whole heap of dissatisfaction comes to arise."

### Ageing And Dying

"And what, bhikkhus, is ageing and dying?

Whatever ageing, wearing out, brokenness of teeth, greying of hair, wrinkling
of skin, decline of life, and maturing of the faculties there is for the
various beings in the various classes of beings: this is called ageing.

Whatever passing away, vanishing, breaking up, disappearance, dying, death,
time-ending, breakup of the heaps, and laying down of the corpse there is for
the various beings in the various classes of beings: this is called death.

Such is ageing, and such is death. This is called ageing and dying."

### Birth

"And what, bhikkhus, is birth?

Whatever birth, being born, descent, appearance, manifestation of the heaps,
and obtaining of the fields there is for the various beings in the various
classes of beings: this is called birth."

### Becoming

"And what, bhikkhus, is becoming?

There are these three kinds of becoming:

- sensual becoming
- form becoming
- formless becoming

This is called becoming."

### Taking Personally

"And what, bhikkhus, is taking personally?

There are these four ways of taking personally:

- sensual clinging
- taking views personally
- clinging to rules and observances
- taking self-doctrine personally

This is called taking personally."

### Ignorant Wanting

"And what, bhikkhus, is ignorant wanting?

There are these six classes of ignorant wanting:

- ignorant wanting for forms
- ignorant wanting for sounds
- ignorant wanting for smells
- ignorant wanting for tastes
- ignorant wanting for tangibles
- ignorant wanting for dhammas

This is called ignorant wanting."

### Felt Experience

"And what, bhikkhus, is felt experience?

There are these six classes of felt experience:

- felt experience born of eye-contact
- felt experience born of ear-contact
- felt experience born of nose-contact
- felt experience born of tongue-contact
- felt experience born of body-contact
- felt experience born of mind-contact

This is called felt experience."

### Contact

"And what, bhikkhus, is contact?

There are these six classes of contact:

- eye-contact
- ear-contact
- nose-contact
- tongue-contact
- body-contact
- mind-contact

This is called contact."

### The Six Fields Of Experience

"And what, bhikkhus, are the six fields of experience?

- the eye sense field
- the ear sense field
- the nose sense field
- the tongue sense field
- the body sense field
- the mind sense field

These are called the six fields of experience."

### Name-And-Form

"And what, bhikkhus, is name-and-form?

- felt experience
- recognition
- intention
- contact
- attention

This is called name.

The four great elements, and form derived from the four great elements: this
is called form.

Such is name, and such is form. This is called name-and-form."

### Knowing

"And what, bhikkhus, is knowing?

There are these six classes of knowing:

- eye-knowing
- ear-knowing
- nose-knowing
- tongue-knowing
- body-knowing
- mind-knowing

This is called knowing."

### Putting Together

"And what, bhikkhus, is putting together?

There are these three kinds of putting together:

- bodily putting together
- verbal putting together
- mental putting together

These are called putting together."

### Ignorance

"And what, bhikkhus, is ignorance?

Not knowing about dissatisfaction, the origin of dissatisfaction, the
quenching of dissatisfaction, and the practice leading to the quenching of
dissatisfaction.

This is called ignorance."

### The Forward And Reverse Summary

"And so, bhikkhus, with ignorance as condition, putting together;
with putting together as condition, knowing;
with knowing as condition, name-and-form;
with name-and-form as condition, the six fields of experience;
with the six fields of experience as condition, contact;
with contact as condition, felt experience;
with felt experience as condition, ignorant wanting;
with ignorant wanting as condition, taking personally;
with taking personally as condition, becoming;
with becoming as condition, birth;
with birth as condition, ageing and dying, sorrow, lamentation,
dissatisfaction, distress, and despair come to be.

That is how this whole heap of dissatisfaction comes to arise.

But with the complete fading and quenching of ignorance, the quenching of
putting together;
with the quenching of putting together, the quenching of knowing;
with the quenching of knowing, the quenching of name-and-form;
with the quenching of name-and-form, the quenching of the six fields of
experience;
with the quenching of the six fields of experience, the quenching of contact;
with the quenching of contact, the quenching of felt experience;
with the quenching of felt experience, the quenching of ignorant wanting;
with the quenching of ignorant wanting, the quenching of taking personally;
with the quenching of taking personally, the quenching of becoming;
with the quenching of becoming, the quenching of birth;
with the quenching of birth, the quenching of ageing and dying, sorrow,
lamentation, dissatisfaction, distress, and despair.

That is how this whole heap of dissatisfaction quenches."

That is what the Buddha said. The bhikkhus were glad and welcomed what the
Buddha had said.

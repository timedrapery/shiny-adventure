# SN 22.59 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[SN 22.59: Anattalakkhaṇa Sutta](sn22-59-anattalakkhana-sutta.md). The main
translation is the primary study surface; this file records debated
translation choices, source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of SN 22.59 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `khandha`, `rūpa`, `vedanā`, `saññā`, `saṅkhārā`, `viññāṇa`, `anicca`,
  `dukkha`, `anattā`, `nibbidā`, `virāga`, `vimutti`, `āsava`, and the
  standard selfing and de-appropriation formulas.
- Working method: the five heaps, the not-self argument, the
  impermanent-to-dissatisfactory-to-not-self question sequence, the standard
  de-appropriation refrain, and the release declaration are governed together
  so the discourse reads as one practical analytical sequence rather than as a
  slogan-source for isolated quotations.

## Governing Decisions

- `Anattalakkhaṇa` is rendered `The Characteristic of Not-Self` in the title
  so the heading stays analytic rather than sermonic or polemical.
- `khandha` family language remains `heap` throughout.
- `rūpa` remains `form`.
- `vedanā` remains `felt experience`.
- `saññā` remains `recognition`.
- `saṅkhārā` remains `putting-together activities` in the heap-family context.
- `viññāṇa` remains `knowing`.
- `anattā` remains `not-self`.
- The standard formula `etaṁ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā` remains
  `This is mine, I am this, this is my self.`
- The standard formula `netaṁ mama, nesohamasmi, na me so attā` remains
  `This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.`

## Re-audit Calls

- The source abbreviates parts of the middle heap sequence, but this study
  edition lays the five heaps out explicitly because the whole family is a
  current control surface in the repository.
- `ābādhāya saṁvattati` is rendered `leads to affliction`. The point is that
  what is being taken as self is liable to trouble and cannot be governed at
  will.
- The question line keeps `dukkha` as `dissatisfaction`, but the following
  predicate line uses the adjective-grade `dissatisfactory` because the
  sentence shape requires it. This does not alter the house default.
- `vipariṇāmadhamma` is rendered `subject to change` rather than given a more
  elevated abstract gloss.
- `sammappaññāya` is rendered `with right discernment` so the refrain stays
  tied to the repository's `paññā` policy.
- The closing `anupādāya` line is rendered `through not taking anything
  personally` so the release close stays aligned with the `upādāna` family and
  with the live de-appropriation handling already used in MN 148.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat SN 22.59 as a metaphysical proof-text about
  whether some hidden self exists behind the heaps.
- The discourse is handled as a practical analysis: what leads to affliction,
  cannot be governed at will, is impermanent, is dissatisfactory, and is
  therefore not fit to be appropriated as self.
- The three-part de-appropriation formula matters because it unpacks not-self
  in practical terms: not mine, not-I-am, not my self.
- The release section matters because not-self is not left as a viewpoint. It
  leads through disenchantment and fading to release.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can
  function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- The five heaps are kept fully explicit because the whole family is the point
  of the discourse.
- The de-appropriation refrain is likewise kept fully explicit because this
  text is one of the repository's key control passages for not-self language.

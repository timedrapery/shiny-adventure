# MN 18 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 18: Madhupiṇḍika Sutta](mn18-madhupindika-sutta.md). The main translation
is the primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 18 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `phassa`, `vedanā`, `saññā`, `sañjānāti`, `vitakka`, `papañca`,
  `papañcasaññāsaṅkhā`, `viññāṇa`, `anusaya`, `kāma`, `bhava`, and `nidāna`.
- Working method: the summary line, the six-door explanatory sequence, and the
  designation sequence are governed together so the sutta reads as one
  practical pattern instead of a set of disconnected doctrinal snippets.

## Governing Decisions

- `Madhupiṇḍika` is rendered `Honey Ball` in the title because the final simile
  is kept close to the source image rather than flattened into a generic sweet
  metaphor.
- `phassa` remains `contact`.
- `vedanā` remains `felt experience`.
- `saññā` remains `recognition`, and `sañjānāti` remains `recognizes`.
- `vitakka` remains `thinking`.
- `papañca` remains `proliferation`.
- `papañcasaññāsaṅkhā` is governed here as
  `recognitions and notions of proliferation`.
- `viññāṇa` remains `knowing`, including `eye-knowing` and `mind-knowing`.
- `dhamma` remains untranslated where the English would otherwise force a
  premature narrowing.
- The `paññatti` sequence is rendered with explicit designation-language:
  `it is possible to designate ...`.

## Re-audit Calls

- Daṇḍapāṇi's opening question is kept short and source-facing:
  `What does the ascetic say? What does he declare?` The reply is not recast
  as a modern doctrinal platform statement.
- `saññā nānusenti` in the opening exchange is rendered with underlying
  recognition-language rather than turned into opinion-language or abstract
  cognition-language.
- `bhavābhave vītataṇhaṁ` is rendered with becoming-language rather than
  imported rebirth rhetoric. The line stays open to the repo's broader
  practical handling of `bhava`.
- `ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṁ abhivaditabbaṁ ajjhositabbaṁ` is rendered
  `if there is nothing there to delight in, to affirm, or to take personally`.
  The point is not merely emotional liking. The line marks a practical cut in
  the movement into appropriation and proliferation.
- `cakkhuñca paṭicca rūpe ca uppajjati cakkhuviññāṇaṁ` is rendered
  `dependent on eye and visible forms, eye-knowing arises` so the eye-door
  formula stays aligned with the repository's current `rūpa` policy for
  sense-field contexts.
- `yatonidānaṁ ... papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti` is governed by the new MN
  18 phrase records rather than left as a document-local improvisation.
- The six-door sequence keeps the full repo chain visible:
  `contact -> felt experience -> recognition -> thinking -> proliferation`.
  This is one of the most important reasons the sutta matters in this
  repository.
- The `paññatti` sequence is not recast as `evidence of` or `concept of`.
  This edition keeps the source-facing designation force visible even though it
  reads a little more technically in English.
- The middle four sense-door repetitions are compacted once the eye pattern is
  fully established and before the mind pattern is restated in full.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat MN 18 as an abstract theory of concept
  formation. It is handled as a practical discourse about how trouble spreads
  through the lived sense process.
- The key point is not only that one recognizes. The discourse shows how
  recognition becomes thinking, and how thinking can become proliferation.
- The summary line is therefore not handled as a condemnation of all thinking.
  It is about what happens when the process is fed rather than understood.
- The line about there being nothing to delight in, affirm, or take personally
  is a practical stopping point, not an ornamental doctrinal slogan.
- The six-door treatment matters because the process is not confined to visual
  thought. The same structure applies across the whole field of contact.
- Mahākaccāna's explanation is also why the repo now treats `papañca` and
  `papañcasaññāsaṅkhā` as governed translation objects rather than leaving them
  as thin glossary residues.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can function
  as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- Repeated sense-door material is partly compacted for readability once the
  governing pattern has been made explicit.
- This companion file should preserve rationale for contested renderings,
  source-audit judgments, and future edition decisions rather than restating
  the entire main text.

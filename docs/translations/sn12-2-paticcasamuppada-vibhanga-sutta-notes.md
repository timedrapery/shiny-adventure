# SN 12.2 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[SN 12.2: Paṭiccasamuppāda-vibhaṅga Sutta](sn12-2-paticcasamuppada-vibhanga-sutta.md).
The main translation is the primary study surface; this file records debated
translation choices, source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of SN 12.2 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `paticcasamuppada`, `paccaya`, `nirodha`, `avijja`, `sankhara`, `vinnana`,
  `namarupa`, `salayatana`, `phassa`, `vedana`, `tanha`, `upadana`, `bhava`,
  `jati`, `jaramarana`, `asesa-viraga-nirodha`, and the generated
  dependent-arising control sheets.
- Working method: the opening chain, the reverse-order definitions, and the
  closing forward-and-reverse summary are governed together so SN 12.2
  functions as one control passage for the dependent-arising family rather
  than as a loose store of reusable fragments.

## Governing Decisions

- `Paṭiccasamuppāda-vibhaṅga` is rendered `Analysis of Dependent Arising`.
- `paṭiccasamuppāda` remains `dependent arising`.
- `paccaya` remains `condition`, and the forward-link sequence keeps the
  stable frame `with X as condition, Y`.
- `saṅkhārā` in the chain remains `putting things together`, not `choices` or
  `volitional formations`.
- `viññāṇa` remains `knowing`.
- `nāmarūpa` remains `name-and-form`.
- `saḷāyatana` remains `six fields of experience`.
- `vedanā` remains `felt experience`.
- `taṇhā` remains `ignorant wanting`.
- `upādāna` remains `taking personally`.
- `jāti` remains `birth`.
- `nirodha` remains `quenching`, including in the reverse chain and the final
  close.

## Re-audit Calls

- The source abbreviates the later forward and reverse chain with ellipsis.
  This study edition lays both sequences out explicitly because SN 12.2 is now
  a live formula-control surface for the repository.
- `dukkhakkhandha` is rendered `this whole heap of dissatisfaction`, aligned
  with the existing `paticcasamuppada` headword example and with the repo's
  `dukkha` policy.
- The `jātipaccayā jarāmaraṇaṁ ...` line keeps `birth` rather than narrowing
  to `rebirth`, in line with the current dependent-arising brief.
- The `upādāna` definition uses the governed compound defaults now active in
  the fourfold framework: `sensual clinging`, `taking views personally`,
  `clinging to rules and observances`, and `taking self-doctrine personally`.
- The `taṇhākāyā` list keeps the headword audible as `ignorant wanting` rather
  than reverting to generic craving-language by habit.
- `nāma` is rendered as `felt experience, recognition, intention, contact, and
  attention` so the line stays aligned with the current `vedana`, `sanna`,
  `cetana`, `phassa`, and `manasikara` entries.
- `cattāro mahābhūtā` is rendered `the four great elements`, using the current
  repo term without importing heavier scholastic phrasing.
- The reverse chain keeps the compact `with the quenching of X, the quenching
  of Y` pattern so the nirodha-family wording does not drift into generic
  cessation-language.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat SN 12.2 as a cosmological map detached from
  present experience.
- The discourse is handled as an analysis of the arising and quenching of
  dissatisfaction through lawful conditional process.
- The `phassa -> vedana -> tanha -> upadana` movement remains the practical
  pressure point of the surface and should stay readable as such.
- The reverse-order definitions matter because the discourse does not merely
  state the chain; it tells the reader what the named links mean.
- The closing reverse chain matters because quenching is not an appendix to
  dependent arising. It is built into the same governed process.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can
  function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- The opening and closing chain sequences are both kept explicit because this
  sutta is now a primary formula-control passage.
- The numbered families inside the definitions are laid out as lists where
  that improves readability without changing the doctrinal sequence.

# MN 7 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 7: Vattha Sutta](mn7-vattha-sutta.md). The main translation is the primary
study surface; this file records debated translation choices, source-audit
calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 7 as segmented in SuttaCentral's Bilara
  data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially `citta`,
  `kilesa`, `upakkilesa`, `aveccappasada`, `saddha`, `pasada`, `buddha`,
  `dhamma`, `sangha`, `sila`, `metta`, `karuna`, `mudita`, `upekkha`, and
  `asava`.
- Working method: the discourse was governed as the repo's first full
  cleansing-and-corruption surface, with DN 2 and MN 99 used for stock formula
  and divine-abiding style controls.

## Governing Decisions

- `citta` remains `feeling mind`.
- `cittassa upakkilesa` is rendered `corruption of the feeling mind`.
- `upakkilesa` remains `corruption`, preserving the narrower surface distinct
  from the broader `kilesa` rendering `defilement`.
- `aveccappasada` remains `verified confidence`.
- `asava` remains `outflow`; the named compounds stay `sensual outflow`,
  `outflow of becoming`, and `outflow of ignorance`.
- `metta`, `karuna`, `mudita`, and `upekkha` remain `friendliness`, `kindness
  towards others`, `gladness`, and `dynamic balance`.
- The Buddha, Dhamma, and Sangha recollection formulas follow the existing DN 2
  voice where possible, with only local adjustments needed by the MN 7 frame.

## Corruption List

- `byapada` is rendered `ill will` in this list. The major-entry default
  `aversive distraction` is retained for the five-distractions framework, but
  this context is an ethical corruption list rather than a nivarana list.
- `kodha`, `upanaha`, `makkha`, `mada`, and `pamada` follow their reviewed
  supporting records: `anger`, `resentment`, `contempt`, `arrogant
  intoxication`, and `heedlessness`.
- `abhijjhavisamalobha`, `palasa`, `issa`, `macchariya`, `maya`, `satheyya`,
  `thambha`, `sarambha`, `mana`, and `atimana` are rendered in practical
  psychological English: `covetous distorted greed`, `rivalry`, `envy`,
  `stinginess`, `deceit`, `fraudulence`, `stubbornness`, `contentious
  aggression`, `conceit`, and `inflated conceit`.
- No new term records were added in this pass. The list is now visible enough
  that a later supporting-family refinement can promote the recurring items
  that prove useful beyond MN 7.

## Cleansing Imagery

- The opening cloth simile keeps `stained and soiled cloth`, matching the
  repo's practical image register and avoiding prestige diction.
- `durattavanna` and `surattavanna` are rendered `badly dyed` and `well dyed`
  so the dyeing image stays concrete.
- The gold-furnace and clear-water comparison remains short because its job is
  to support the food-obstacle statement, not to create a new metallurgy
  control surface.
- `sinato antarena sinanena` is rendered `bathed by inner bathing`, keeping
  the bathing contrast audible before the brahmin's river question.

## Formula And Practice Calls

- `atthaveda`, `dhammaveda`, and `dhammupasamhita pamojja` are rendered `a
  sense of the meaning`, `a sense of the Dhamma`, and `gladness connected with
  the Dhamma`, following the established MN 99 style.
- `piti` is rendered `delight` in the gladness-to-composure sequence, matching
  the current translation voice for positive meditative affect.
- `sabbattataya` is rendered `to all as to oneself`, preserving the universal
  scope without turning the phrase into a separate doctrine.
- `atthi idam, atthi hinam, atthi panitam, atthi imassa sannagatassa uttari
  nissaranam` is rendered as a compact discernment line: `There is this. There
  is what is low. There is what is refined. There is an escape beyond this
  field reached by recognition.` This keeps `sanna` connected to the
  repository's recognition policy while marking the phrase as a local
  interpretive pressure point for future review.

## Editorial Presentation

- The repeated abandoning list is mostly expanded, because MN 7 is now the
  outward-facing list surface for the corruptions of the feeling mind.
- The second full repetition, after the corruptions have been abandoned, is
  compressed into one paragraph to keep the document readable while preserving
  every list member.
- Verse lines are broken for readability, but the meaning is kept direct and
  source-facing.

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first stable study edition of the repo's
  MN 7 translation surface.
- Future revisions should focus on the corruption-list family, the `sannagata`
  escape line, or stock-formula synchronization if those areas gain stronger
  repository controls.

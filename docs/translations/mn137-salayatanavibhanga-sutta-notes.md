# MN 137 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 137: Saḷāyatanavibhaṅga Sutta](mn137-salayatanavibhanga-sutta.md). The main
translation is the primary study surface; this file records debated
translation choices, source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 137 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially `āyatana`,
  `saḷāyatana`, `phassa`, `vedanā`-adjacent feeling families, `manopavicāra`,
  `somanassa`, `domanassa`, `upekkhā`, `nekkhamma`, `satipaṭṭhāna`,
  `atammayatā`, and the formless-field family.
- Working method: the sixfold analytic list, the household / renunciation
  affective families, the staged replacement formula, and the closing
  teacher-balance section are governed together so the discourse reads as one
  practical system rather than as four disconnected mini-suttas.

## Governing Decisions

- `Saḷāyatanavibhaṅga` is rendered `Analysis of the Six Fields`.
- `āyatana` remains `field`, including the internal and external sixfold lists.
- `manopavicāra` remains `mental exploration`.
- `somanassa` remains `gladness`.
- `domanassa` remains `distress`.
- `upekkhā` remains `dynamic balance`, including in the household /
  renunciation and diversity / oneness sections.
- `nekkhammasita` and `gehasita` are rendered here as `tied to renunciation`
  and `tied to the household life` rather than flattened into generic pleasant
  and unpleasant categories.
- `satipaṭṭhāna` remains `establishment of sati` even in the closing
  threefold teacher-response section.
- `atammayatā` remains `non-identification`.

## Re-audit Calls

- `manopavicāra` is rendered `mental exploration` so the line keeps active
  mental turning rather than drifting into mere thought-language.
- `tatra idaṁ nissāya idaṁ pajahatha` is now governed by an explicit phrase
  record and stays direct: `here, supported by this, give up that`.
- The renunciation-side joy, distress, and dynamic-balance formulas are kept
  visibly tied to seeing inconstancy, dissatisfaction, and change with
  discernment. They are not softened into vague spiritual uplift.
- `anodhijinassa avipākajinassa` in the household dynamic-balance section is
  still partly source-facing here as `has not conquered the bounds or the
  results`. That line remains a likely future policy-tightening point if this
  family recurs elsewhere in the corpus.
- `nānattā nānattasitā` and `ekattā ekattasitā` are rendered as dynamic
  balance `tied to diversity` and `tied to oneness`. This keeps the structural
  contrast audible without importing later technical jargon.
- The closing `tayo satipaṭṭhānā` line now has an explicit phrase record so the
  discourse does not drift back into mindfulness-foundation language.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat MN 137 as a dry sensory taxonomy.
- The discourse maps how the six fields open into gladness, distress, and
  dynamic balance, and then shows how coarser patterns are to be given up by
  leaning on subtler supports.
- The renunciation-side distress is deliberate and important. It is not a
  failure state. It is the pain of longing for release before release is fully
  entered.
- The household dynamic-balance section matters because the discourse does not
  allow every calm-looking state to count as liberating. Some balance remains
  bounded by the object and does not go beyond it.
- The closing teacher section matters because it shows a different practical
  use of satipaṭṭhāna: stable, non-drifting response to how disciples receive
  instruction.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can
  function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- The sixfold lists are given explicitly where the inventory itself matters.
- The middle sense-door repetitions inside longer affective formulas are
  compacted once the eye-side pattern is established and the mind-side pattern
  is restated.
- The final eight-direction sequence is presented as a readable list rather
  than as a paragraph block.

## Next Refinement Areas

- If more feeling-domain translations are added, the `gehasita` /
  `nekkhammasita` family deserves dedicated upstream records rather than
  remaining mostly translation-local.
- The `anodhijinassa avipākajinassa` line in the household dynamic-balance
  section deserves a stronger lexical decision if the same idiom recurs in a
  later text.
- If the final satipaṭṭhāna section becomes an important control passage for
  teacher-response language, the `satipaṭṭhāna` major record may need a narrow
  MN 137 context rule rather than relying only on the new phrase record.

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first stable study edition of the repo's
  MN 137 translation surface.
- Future revisions should be driven by source-audit gains, term-policy
  expansion, or real readability improvements, not routine wording churn.

# MN 141 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 141: Saccavibhaṅga Sutta](mn141-saccavibhanga-sutta.md). The main
translation is the primary study surface; this file records debated
translation choices, source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 141 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `ariyasacca`, `sacca`, `dukkha`, `samudaya`, `nirodha`, `magga`, `taṇhā`,
  `nandi`, `mutti`, `jāti`, `jarāmaraṇa`, `soka`, `parideva`, `domanassa`,
  `upāyāsa`, `pancupadanakkhandha`, `sammā-ditthi`, `sammā-saṅkappa`,
  `sammā-sati`, `sammā-samādhi`, and the MN 10 / MN 117 translation surfaces.
- Working method: the opening truth-analysis formula, the four truth
  definitions, and the eightfold-path section are governed together so the
  discourse reads as one explicit analytical exposition rather than as a
  stitched set of familiar quotations.

## Governing Decisions

- `Saccavibhaṅga` is rendered `Analysis of the Truths`.
- `ariyasacca` remains `noble truth`.
- `dukkha` remains `dissatisfaction` at the truth level, while the narrower
  `dukkhaṁ` member in the first-truth expansion is rendered `pain`.
- `samudaya` remains `origin`.
- `nirodha` remains `quenching`.
- `magga` remains `path`.
- The analytical opening formula is now governed by an explicit phrase record
  instead of being left as document-only wording.
- The third-truth tail around `cāgo paṭinissaggo mutti anālayo` is also now
  governed by an explicit phrase record.
- `sammā-sati` remains `right remembering`, and `sammā-samādhi` remains `right
  mental composure`.
- `jhāna` remains `mental theme`, in line with the live jhāna-family policy.

## Re-audit Calls

- `dhammacakkaṁ pavattitaṁ appaṭivattiyaṁ` is rendered with `set rolling` and
  `cannot be rolled back` to keep the line forceful without sliding into
  scriptural cadence.
- `catunnaṁ ariyasaccānaṁ ācikkhanā desanā paññāpanā paṭṭhapanā vivaraṇā
  vibhajanā uttānīkammaṁ` is translated as a multi-verb analytical sequence
  rather than a single abstract noun such as exposition.
- The first-truth summary keeps `sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and
  despair` aligned with the current repo distinction between bodily pain,
  mental distress, and despair.
- `yaṁpicchaṁ na labhati tampi dukkhaṁ` is kept plain: `not getting what one
  wants is also dissatisfaction`.
- The second-truth line stays aligned with the existing taṇhā family:
  `ignorant wanting`, `relishing and passion`, and `delighting here and there`.
- The third-truth tail stays coordinated as `giving it up, relinquishing it,
  freedom, non-attachment` rather than collapsing into one release-word.
- The right-remembering section reuses the current MN 10 house wording.
- The right-mental-composure section reuses the current jhāna-language already
  stabilized in the live corpus.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition treats MN 141 as an explicit analytical restatement of the four
  noble truths, not as a separate doctrinal system.
- The discourse matters because it spreads the first truth out into named,
  workable components rather than leaving it as a slogan.
- The path section matters because it anchors the four truths back into
  trainable conduct, attitude, remembering, and mental composure.
- The truth-analysis opening is not ornamental. It tells the reader what kind
  of discourse this is: one that explains, lays out, and makes clear.
- Sāriputta's role matters. The Buddha points to him as the one able to carry
  the detailed analysis forward after the wheel of Dhamma has been set rolling.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can
  function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- The first-truth expansion is kept explicit rather than compressed because it
  functions as a core control passage.
- The eightfold path is likewise kept explicit because it anchors current
  headword policy across several major entries.

## Next Refinement Areas

- If more four-truths translations are added, the first-truth summary line may
  deserve its own explicit phrase record rather than being governed only by the
  current family of headwords.
- The `cāga` minor record is still thinner than the release-sequence work now
  being done around it. A future pass could sharpen it for four-truths usage.
- If more Sariputta analytical discourses are translated, a small stock-phrase
  layer around the `ācikkhanā / desanā / paññāpanā` family may be worthwhile.

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first stable study edition of the repo's
  MN 141 translation surface.
- Future revisions should be driven by source-audit gains, term-policy
  expansion, or real readability improvements, not routine wording churn.

## Translation

### The Setting

So I heard.

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī, in Jeta's Grove, in
Anāthapiṇḍika's monastery.

There Venerable Sāriputta addressed the bhikkhus:

"Friends, bhikkhus."

"Friend," those bhikkhus replied.

Venerable Sāriputta said this:

### The Question About Right View

"Friends, people say, 'right view, right view.'

In what way, friends, is a noble disciple one with right view, whose view has
become direct, who is endowed with verified confidence in the Dhamma, and who
has arrived at this true Dhamma?"

"Friend, we would come from far away to learn the meaning of this statement in
Venerable Sāriputta's presence. Let its meaning occur to Venerable Sāriputta
himself. Hearing it from Venerable Sāriputta, the bhikkhus will remember it."

"Then, friends, listen carefully, attend well, and I will speak."

"Yes, friend," the bhikkhus replied.

Venerable Sāriputta said this:

### Wholesome and Unwholesome Roots

"Friends, when a noble disciple discerns the unwholesome, discerns the root of
the unwholesome, discerns the wholesome, and discerns the root of the
wholesome, to that extent the noble disciple is one with right view, whose view
has become direct, who is endowed with verified confidence in the Dhamma, and
who has arrived at this true Dhamma.

And what is the unwholesome? What is the root of the unwholesome? What is the
wholesome? What is the root of the wholesome?

Taking life is unwholesome.
Taking what is not given is unwholesome.
Sexual misconduct is unwholesome.
False speech is unwholesome.
Divisive speech is unwholesome.
Harsh speech is unwholesome.
Idle chatter is unwholesome.
Coveting is unwholesome.
Ill will is unwholesome.
Wrong view is unwholesome.

This is called the unwholesome.

And what is the root of the unwholesome?

Greed is a root of the unwholesome.
Aversion is a root of the unwholesome.
Delusion is a root of the unwholesome.

This is called the root of the unwholesome.

And what is the wholesome?

Abstaining from taking life is wholesome.
Abstaining from taking what is not given is wholesome.
Abstaining from sexual misconduct is wholesome.
Abstaining from false speech is wholesome.
Abstaining from divisive speech is wholesome.
Abstaining from harsh speech is wholesome.
Abstaining from idle chatter is wholesome.
Non-coveting is wholesome.
Non-ill-will is wholesome.
Right view is wholesome.

This is called the wholesome.

And what is the root of the wholesome?

Non-greed is a root of the wholesome.
Non-aversion is a root of the wholesome.
Clarity is a root of the wholesome.

This is called the root of the wholesome.

When a noble disciple discerns the unwholesome in this way, discerns the root
of the unwholesome in this way, discerns the wholesome in this way, and
discerns the root of the wholesome in this way, then, having abandoned the
underlying tendency toward passion entirely, having dispelled the underlying
tendency toward resistance, having uprooted the underlying tendency to the view
and conceit 'I am,' having abandoned ignorance and given rise to clear
knowledge, he makes an end of dissatisfaction in this very life.

To that extent, too, the noble disciple is one with right view, whose view has
become direct, who is endowed with verified confidence in the Dhamma, and who
has arrived at this true Dhamma."

### Another Way

The bhikkhus were glad and approved of Venerable Sāriputta's words. Then they
asked him a further question:

"But, friend, might there be another way in which a noble disciple is one with
right view, whose view has become direct, who is endowed with verified
confidence in the Dhamma, and who has arrived at this true Dhamma?"

"There might, friends.

### Nutriment

When a noble disciple discerns nutriment, discerns the origin of nutriment,
discerns the quenching of nutriment, and discerns the path of practice leading
to the quenching of nutriment, to that extent the noble disciple is one with
right view, whose view has become direct, who is endowed with verified
confidence in the Dhamma, and who has arrived at this true Dhamma.

And what is nutriment? What is the origin of nutriment? What is the quenching
of nutriment? What is the path of practice leading to the quenching of
nutriment?

There are these four nutriments for the maintenance of beings who have come to
be, and for the support of those seeking to come to be.

Which four?

- edible-food nutriment, whether coarse or subtle
- contact as the second
- mental intention as the third
- knowing as the fourth

With the origin of ignorant wanting, the origin of nutriment comes to be. With
the quenching of ignorant wanting, the quenching of nutriment comes to be.

This same noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching
of nutriment:

- right view
- right attitude
- right speech
- right action
- right livelihood
- right effort
- right remembering
- right mental composure

When a noble disciple discerns nutriment in this way, discerns the origin of
nutriment in this way, discerns the quenching of nutriment in this way, and
discerns the path of practice leading to the quenching of nutriment in this
way, then, having abandoned the underlying tendency toward passion entirely,
having dispelled the underlying tendency toward resistance, having uprooted the
underlying tendency to the view and conceit 'I am,' having abandoned ignorance
and given rise to clear knowledge, he makes an end of dissatisfaction in this
very life.

To that extent, too, the noble disciple is one with right view, whose view has
become direct, who is endowed with verified confidence in the Dhamma, and who
has arrived at this true Dhamma."

### The Noble Truths

Again the bhikkhus approved and asked whether there was another way. Sāriputta
said:

"There might, friends.

When a noble disciple discerns dissatisfaction, discerns the origin of
dissatisfaction, discerns the quenching of dissatisfaction, and discerns the
path of practice leading to the quenching of dissatisfaction, to that extent
the noble disciple is one with right view.

And what is dissatisfaction?

Birth is dissatisfaction.
Ageing is dissatisfaction.
Death is dissatisfaction.
Sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and despair are dissatisfaction.
Association with what is disliked is dissatisfaction.
Separation from what is liked is dissatisfaction.
Not getting what one wants is dissatisfaction.
In brief, the five clung-to heaps are dissatisfaction.

This is called dissatisfaction.

And what is the origin of dissatisfaction?

That ignorant wanting that leads to renewed becoming, accompanied by relishing
and passion, delighting here and there. That is:

- ignorant wanting for sensuality
- ignorant wanting for becoming
- ignorant wanting for non-becoming

This is called the origin of dissatisfaction.

And what is the quenching of dissatisfaction?

The complete fading and quenching of that very ignorant wanting: giving it up,
relinquishing it, freedom, non-attachment.

This is called the quenching of dissatisfaction.

And what is the path of practice leading to the quenching of dissatisfaction?

It is just this noble eightfold path:

- right view
- right attitude
- right speech
- right action
- right livelihood
- right effort
- right remembering
- right mental composure

This is called the path of practice leading to the quenching of
dissatisfaction.

When a noble disciple discerns this in this way, then, having abandoned the
underlying tendency toward passion entirely, having dispelled the underlying
tendency toward resistance, having uprooted the underlying tendency to the view
and conceit 'I am,' having abandoned ignorance and given rise to clear
knowledge, he makes an end of dissatisfaction in this very life.

To that extent, too, the noble disciple is one with right view."

### The Dependent-Arising Analyses

Again the bhikkhus approved and asked whether there was another way.

For each analysis that follows, Sāriputta gives the same right-view frame: a
noble disciple discerns the term, its origin, its quenching, and the path of
practice leading to its quenching. Discerning in this way, the noble disciple
abandons the underlying tendency toward passion, dispels the underlying
tendency toward resistance, uproots the underlying tendency to the view and
conceit "I am," abandons ignorance, gives rise to clear knowledge, and makes an
end of dissatisfaction in this very life. To that extent, too, the noble
disciple is one with right view.

### Ageing and Dying

What is ageing and dying?

Whatever ageing, wearing out, brokenness of teeth, greying of hair, wrinkling
of skin, decline of life, and maturing of the faculties there is for the
various beings in the various classes of beings: this is called ageing.

Whatever passing away, vanishing, breaking up, disappearance, dying, death,
time-ending, breakup of the heaps, laying down of the corpse, and cutting off
of the life faculty there is for the various beings in the various classes of
beings: this is called death.

Such is ageing, and such is death. This is called ageing and dying.

With the origin of birth, the origin of ageing and dying comes to be. With the
quenching of birth, the quenching of ageing and dying comes to be. This same
noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching of ageing
and dying.

### Birth

What is birth?

Whatever birth, being born, descent, appearance, manifestation of the heaps,
and obtaining of the fields there is for the various beings in the various
classes of beings: this is called birth.

With the origin of becoming, the origin of birth comes to be. With the
quenching of becoming, the quenching of birth comes to be. This same noble
eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching of birth.

### Becoming

What is becoming?

There are these three kinds of becoming:

- sensual becoming
- form becoming
- formless becoming

This is called becoming.

With the origin of taking personally, the origin of becoming comes to be. With
the quenching of taking personally, the quenching of becoming comes to be. This
same noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching of
becoming.

### Taking Personally

What is taking personally?

There are these four ways of taking personally:

- sensual clinging
- taking views personally
- clinging to rules and observances
- taking self-doctrine personally

This is called taking personally.

With the origin of ignorant wanting, the origin of taking personally comes to
be. With the quenching of ignorant wanting, the quenching of taking personally
comes to be. This same noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to
the quenching of taking personally.

### Ignorant Wanting

What is ignorant wanting?

There are these six classes of ignorant wanting:

- ignorant wanting for forms
- ignorant wanting for sounds
- ignorant wanting for smells
- ignorant wanting for tastes
- ignorant wanting for tangibles
- ignorant wanting for dhammas

This is called ignorant wanting.

With the origin of felt experience, the origin of ignorant wanting comes to be.
With the quenching of felt experience, the quenching of ignorant wanting comes
to be. This same noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the
quenching of ignorant wanting.

### Felt Experience

What is felt experience?

There are these six classes of felt experience:

- felt experience born of eye-contact
- felt experience born of ear-contact
- felt experience born of nose-contact
- felt experience born of tongue-contact
- felt experience born of body-contact
- felt experience born of mind-contact

This is called felt experience.

With the origin of contact, the origin of felt experience comes to be. With the
quenching of contact, the quenching of felt experience comes to be. This same
noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching of felt
experience.

### Contact

What is contact?

There are these six classes of contact:

- eye-contact
- ear-contact
- nose-contact
- tongue-contact
- body-contact
- mind-contact

This is called contact.

With the origin of the six fields of experience, the origin of contact comes to
be. With the quenching of the six fields of experience, the quenching of
contact comes to be. This same noble eightfold path is the path of practice
leading to the quenching of contact.

### The Six Fields of Experience

What are the six fields of experience?

There are these six fields:

- the eye sense field
- the ear sense field
- the nose sense field
- the tongue sense field
- the body sense field
- the mind sense field

These are called the six fields of experience.

With the origin of name-and-form, the origin of the six fields of experience
comes to be. With the quenching of name-and-form, the quenching of the six
fields of experience comes to be. This same noble eightfold path is the path of
practice leading to the quenching of the six fields of experience.

### Name-And-Form

What is name-and-form?

- felt experience
- recognition
- intention
- contact
- attention

This is called name.

The four great elements, and form derived from the four great elements: this is
called form.

Such is name, and such is form. This is called name-and-form.

With the origin of knowing, the origin of name-and-form comes to be. With the
quenching of knowing, the quenching of name-and-form comes to be. This same
noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching of
name-and-form.

### Knowing

What is knowing?

There are these six classes of knowing:

- eye-knowing
- ear-knowing
- nose-knowing
- tongue-knowing
- body-knowing
- mind-knowing

This is called knowing.

With the origin of putting things together, the origin of knowing comes to be.
With the quenching of putting things together, the quenching of knowing comes
to be. This same noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the
quenching of knowing.

### Putting Things Together

What are putting things together?

There are these three kinds of putting things together:

- bodily putting things together
- verbal putting things together
- mental putting things together

These are called putting things together.

With the origin of ignorance, the origin of putting things together comes to
be. With the quenching of ignorance, the quenching of putting things together
comes to be. This same noble eightfold path is the path of practice leading to
the quenching of putting things together.

### Ignorance

What is ignorance?

Not knowing about dissatisfaction, not knowing about the origin of
dissatisfaction, not knowing about the quenching of dissatisfaction, and not
knowing about the path of practice leading to the quenching of dissatisfaction:
this is called ignorance.

With the origin of outflows, the origin of ignorance comes to be. With the
quenching of outflows, the quenching of ignorance comes to be. This same noble
eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching of ignorance.

### Outflows

Again the bhikkhus approved and asked whether there was another way. Sāriputta
said:

"There might, friends.

When a noble disciple discerns outflow, discerns the origin of outflow,
discerns the quenching of outflow, and discerns the path of practice leading to
the quenching of outflow, to that extent the noble disciple is one with right
view, whose view has become direct, who is endowed with verified confidence in
the Dhamma, and who has arrived at this true Dhamma.

And what is outflow? What is the origin of outflow? What is the quenching of
outflow? What is the path of practice leading to the quenching of outflow?

There are these three outflows:

- sensual outflow
- outflow of becoming
- outflow of ignorance

With the origin of ignorance, the origin of outflows comes to be. With the
quenching of ignorance, the quenching of outflows comes to be. This same noble
eightfold path is the path of practice leading to the quenching of outflows:

- right view
- right attitude
- right speech
- right action
- right livelihood
- right effort
- right remembering
- right mental composure

When a noble disciple discerns outflow in this way, discerns the origin of
outflow in this way, discerns the quenching of outflow in this way, and
discerns the path of practice leading to the quenching of outflow in this way,
then, having abandoned the underlying tendency toward passion entirely, having
dispelled the underlying tendency toward resistance, having uprooted the
underlying tendency to the view and conceit 'I am,' having abandoned ignorance
and given rise to clear knowledge, he makes an end of dissatisfaction in this
very life.

To that extent, too, the noble disciple is one with right view, whose view has
become direct, who is endowed with verified confidence in the Dhamma, and who
has arrived at this true Dhamma."

That is what Venerable Sāriputta said.

The bhikkhus were satisfied and welcomed what Venerable Sāriputta had said.

The Sammādiṭṭhi Sutta, the ninth discourse, is finished.

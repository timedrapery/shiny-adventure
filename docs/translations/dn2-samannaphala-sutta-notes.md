# DN 2 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[DN 2: Sāmaññaphala Sutta](dn2-samannaphala-sutta.md). The main translation is
the primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of DN 2 as segmented in SuttaCentral's Bilara
  data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially `sila`,
  `patimokkha`, `samvara`, `indriya-samvara`, `sati-sampajanna`,
  `sampajanna`, `santutthi`, `nivarana`, `panca-nivarana`, `jhana`,
  `pathama-jhana`, `dutiya-jhana`, `tatiya-jhana`, `catuttha-jhana`,
  `vitakka`, `vicara`, `ekaggata`, `samadhi`, `samma-samadhi`,
  `vivekaja-piti-sukha`, `samadhija-piti-sukha`, `upekkha-sukha`,
  `upekkha-satiparisuddha`, `asava`, `abhinna`, `vijja`, and the generated
  jhāna control sheets.
- Working method: the gradual-training lead-in, the clearing of the five
  distractions, the four mental themes, and the higher-knowledge sequence are
  governed together so DN 2 reads as one controlled training arc rather than
  as disconnected stock passages.

## Governing Decisions

- `Sāmaññaphala` is rendered `The Fruits of the Ascetic Life`.
- `sīla` remains `conduct`, not `morality`.
- `pātimokkha` remains untranslated in the core going-forth formula.
- `indriyasaṃvara` is handled through `guarding the faculties`.
- The hindrance-clearing section keeps the collective line as `these five
  distractions` while allowing source-close wording inside the five individual
  abandonings.
- `jhāna` remains `mental theme` throughout the fourfold sequence.
- `samādhi` remains `mental composure`, while `cetaso ekodibhāva` keeps
  `unification of mind` in the second-theme formula.
- The second-theme delight pair is rendered `delight and ease born of mental
  composure` so the formula stays aligned with the live `samādhi` policy and
  existing translation surfaces.
- `abhiññā` remains in higher-knowing territory without spectacle language,
  and `āsava` remains `outflow`.

## Re-audit Calls

- The early narrative is kept plain. The moonlit opening, the royal fear at
  the grove's silence, and Ajātasattu's wish for his son are handled as
  readable scene-setting rather than as ornamental court prose.
- The six rival-teacher sections are translated as doctrinal answers that fail
  the king's practical question. Their repetitive approach-and-withdrawal frame
  is compressed only enough to keep the document readable as one study text.
- The gradual-training turn is the real center of the discourse. The file
  therefore gives full space to conduct, guarding the faculties, clear
  knowing, contentment, distraction-clearing, and the four mental themes.
- The long conduct lists stay plain and modern rather than sounding liturgical
  or pseudo-biblical. DN 2 needs stable training prose more than prestige
  diction.
- The hindrance-clearing lead-in remains visible before the first mental
  theme. The sequence is not flattened into generic meditation-entry language.
- The third and fourth mental themes stay aligned with the current governed
  `dynamic balance`, `ease`, and `purified by dynamic balance and remembering`
  wording.
- The higher-knowledge sequence is kept practical and anti-spectacle. Even
  where the text names extraordinary capacities, the wording avoids occult
  inflation.
- The final outflow-ending sequence stays coordinated with the existing MN 19
  and outflow-family formulas: `dissatisfaction`, `origin`, `quenching`,
  `outflow`, `freed`, and `birth is worn away`.

## Practice Clarifications

- DN 2 does not only answer whether ascetic life has a result. It answers that
  question by moving from visible social fruits to conduct, sense restraint,
  clear knowing, contentment, collected practice, and final release.
- The first two fruits matter because they answer the king in terms he can
  already see. The later fruits matter because they show what the training is
  actually for.
- The discourse is an important control passage for keeping the four mental
  themes inside the lived training sequence rather than turning them into a
  detached altered-state theory.
- The final confession scene matters because the discourse ends not with a
  spectacle of powers but with the king's moral failure, partial opening, and
  the Buddha's sober assessment of the cost of that act.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can function
  as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- Long list material is grouped and formatted for readability, but the
  translation keeps the discourse's full practical arc intact.

# MN 44 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 44: Cūḷavedalla Sutta](mn44-culavedalla-sutta.md). The main translation is
the primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 44 as segmented in SuttaCentral's Bilara
  data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `sakkāya`, `sakkāyadiṭṭhi`, `pañcupādānakkhandhā`, `upādāna`, `chanda`,
  `rāga`, `taṇhā`, `kāmataṇhā`, `bhavataṇhā`, `vibhavataṇhā`, `ariyasāvaka`,
  `sammādiṭṭhi`, `sammāsaṅkappa`, `sammāvācā`, `sammākammanta`,
  `sammāājīva`, `sammāvāyāma`, `sammāsati`, `sammāsamādhi`, `samādhi`,
  `ekaggatā`, `satipaṭṭhāna`, `sammappadhāna`, `kāyasaṅkhāra`,
  `vacīsaṅkhāra`, `cittasaṅkhāra`, `vitakka`, `vicāra`, `vedanā`, `saññā`,
  `sukha-vedanā`, `dukkha-vedanā`, `adukkhamasukha-vedanā`, `anusaya`,
  `rāga-anusaya`, `paṭigha-anusaya`, `avijjā-anusaya`, `jhāna`, `vimutti`,
  `vijjā`, and `nibbāna`.
- Working method: the dialogue was governed as one chain of definitions rather
  than as independent doctrinal snippets, because later answers about
  attainment, felt experience, and release depend on the opening analysis of
  identity and taking personally.

## Governing Decisions

- `sakkāya` is rendered `identity`, not body, personality, or self as an
  abstract doctrine.
- `sakkāyadiṭṭhi` remains `identity view`.
- `pañcupādānakkhandhā` remains `five clung-to heaps`, and the individual
  members are rendered as `clung-to heap of form`, `clung-to heap of felt
  experience`, `clung-to heap of recognition`, `clung-to heap of putting
  things together`, and `clung-to heap of knowing`.
- `upādāna` remains `taking personally`, so the answer about
  `chandarāga` reads as the desire-and-passion movement inside the five
  clung-to heaps rather than as a separate substance called clinging.
- The ignorant-wanting formula stays aligned with SN 56.11, MN 9, and MN 141:
  `that ignorant wanting that leads to renewed becoming, accompanied by
  relishing and passion, delighting here and there`.
- The eight path factors stay aligned with MN 9 and MN 117: `right view`,
  `right attitude`, `right speech`, `right action`, `right livelihood`, `right
  effort`, `right remembering`, and `right mental composure`.
- `saṅkhata` and `asaṅkhata` are rendered `conditioned` and `unconditioned` in
  the question about the noble eightfold path.
- `samādhi` remains `mental composure`; `cittassa ekaggatā` is rendered
  `directness of the feeling mind`.
- `satipaṭṭhāna` is rendered `establishments of sati` in the technical answer
  about signs of mental composure, preserving the repository's refusal of
  mindfulness-language while keeping the framework recognizable.
- `kāyasaṅkhāra`, `vacīsaṅkhāra`, and `cittasaṅkhāra` are rendered `body
  conditioner`, `verbal process`, and `mind conditioner`.
- `saññāvedayitanirodhasamāpatti` is rendered `attainment of cessation of
  recognition and felt experience`. This is a controlled compound-specific use
  of cessation-language and does not alter the default `nirodha` policy of
  `quenching` in the identity and path formulas.
- `sukha-vedanā`, `dukkha-vedanā`, and `adukkhamasukha-vedanā` are rendered
  `pleasant feeling`, `painful feeling`, and `mixed feeling`.
- The release-side close keeps the live sequence `ignorance`, `clear
  knowledge`, `release`, and `nibbāna`.

## Re-audit Calls

- Visākha's repeated respectful address to Dhammadinnā is left as `Ayye`. The
  choice parallels the repository's use of `Bhante` in Buddha-address formulas
  without forcing the feminine vocative into awkward English such as
  `Venerable sir`.
- Dhammadinnā's address `āvuso visākha` is rendered `friend Visākha`, matching
  the repository's handling of peer and non-Buddha dialogue address.
- The opening identity analysis keeps the five clung-to heaps explicit rather
  than reducing the answer to a summary. MN 44 is valuable precisely because it
  says identity is the five clung-to heaps and then distinguishes that from
  taking personally.
- The answer on taking personally is deliberately not rendered as "clinging is
  neither the same as nor different from the aggregates." The local policy
  requires both `upādāna` and `pañcupādānakkhandhā` to stay audible in their
  governed forms.
- The identity-view formula uses `regards ... as self`, following the existing
  `attā` examples, and then expands the fourfold pattern across all five
  heaps instead of leaving the root's ellipsis unresolved for readers.
- `sīlakkhandha`, `samādhikkhandha`, and `paññākkhandha` are rendered
  `conduct heap`, `mental-composure heap`, and `discernment heap`. This keeps
  the `khandha` family visible while avoiding the inherited aggregate-language
  that the repository generally treats as a drift risk.
- The mental-composure answer keeps `signs` for `nimittā` and `equipment` for
  `parikkhārā`. These are document-level renderings because no current term
  record requires a broader policy change.
- The cessation-attainment section uses `quenched` and `arises` for the order
  of the three conditioners, while retaining `attainment of cessation` for the
  technical compound itself.
- `suññato phasso`, `animitto phasso`, and `appaṇihito phasso` are rendered
  `emptiness contact`, `signless contact`, and `wishless contact` so the
  release-side contemplative family stays compact and governed.
- The first and fourth mental-theme passages stay aligned with MN 19: thinking
  and pondering, delight and ease born of seclusion, and purity of remembering
  through dynamic balance are retained rather than converted to concentration
  or absorption language.
- `anuttaresu vimokkhesu pihaṁ upaṭṭhāpayato` is rendered with `longing for
  unsurpassed releases`. The line preserves the strange but important point
  that a painful feeling can be present in a wholesome aspiration without the
  underlying tendency toward resistance lying there.
- `nibbānogadhaṁ`, `nibbānaparāyanaṁ`, and `nibbānapariyosānaṁ` are rendered
  `grounded in nibbāna`, `directed to nibbāna`, and `completed in nibbāna`.
  The sentence is meant to stop the counterpart-question chain without turning
  nibbāna into a further item that needs an opposite.

## Practice Clarifications

- MN 44 is one of the repository's strongest surfaces for distinguishing
  identity from taking personally. The five clung-to heaps are what the Buddha
  calls identity; the desire and passion in regard to them is the taking
  personally there.
- The path is not presented as unconditioned. It is conditioned training that
  leads to the quenching of identity.
- The path factors are also not a fourth structure standing outside conduct,
  mental composure, and discernment. MN 44 explicitly gathers the path factors
  into those three heaps.
- Mental composure is not mere concentration. Dhammadinnā defines it through
  directness of the feeling mind, signs, equipment, and development.
- The three-conditioner section is important for breathing practice because it
  directly connects in-breathing and out-breathing with the body conditioner,
  thinking and pondering with the verbal process, and recognition and felt
  experience with the mind conditioner.
- The cessation-attainment section should not be read as self-command. The
  bhikkhu does not enter or emerge by narrating the state to himself; the
  feeling mind has already been developed in such a way that it leads there.
- The felt-experience section prevents two opposite mistakes: pleasant feeling
  is not automatically a problem, and painful feeling is not automatically
  resistance. The underlying tendency depends on how the feeling is met and on
  the training context.
- The final counterpart sequence matters because it moves from felt experience
  through ignorance, clear knowledge, release, and nibbāna, then refuses to
  make nibbāna another term in an endless chain of opposites.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the question-and-answer
  sequence can function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in the
  root text itself.
- Repeated question formulas are translated fully where they establish a new
  section, while some repeated vocative cadence is kept compact so the dialogue
  remains readable.
- The root's ellipses in the identity-view section are expanded in English
  because the five-heap pattern is the doctrinal point of the passage.

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first stable study edition of the repo's MN
  44 translation surface.
- Future revisions should be driven by source-audit gains, term-policy
  expansion, or real readability improvements, not routine wording churn.

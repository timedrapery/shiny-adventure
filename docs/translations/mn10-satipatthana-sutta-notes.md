# MN 10 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[MN 10: Satipaṭṭhāna Sutta](mn10-satipatthana-sutta.md). The main translation
is the primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of MN 10 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `satipaṭṭhāna`, `sati`, `sampajañña`, `kāya`, `vedanā`, `citta`, `dhamma`,
  `ajjhatta`, `bahiddha`, `nīvaraṇa`, `bojjhaṅga`, `ariyasacca`, `upādāna`,
  and `nibbāna`.
- Working method: the direct-path opening, the branch-defining qualifier line,
  and the repeated refrains are governed together so the discourse reads as one
  practical training system rather than as a pile of meditation fragments.

## Governing Decisions

- `Satipaṭṭhāna` is rendered `Establishment of Sati` in the title and
  `establishment of sati` in the body.
- `sati` remains `remembering`.
- `sampajañña` remains `clear knowing`.
- `vedanā` remains `felt experience`, and the third register is rendered
  `mixed feeling`.
- `citta` remains `feeling mind`.
- `dhamma` remains untranslated in `dhammānupassanā`.
- The five `nīvaraṇa` members follow the current repo family language:
  `sensual distraction`, `aversive distraction`, `dull distraction`,
  `agitated distraction`, and `uncertain distraction`.
- The opening `ekāyano ayaṁ ... maggo` line is governed here as `direct path`,
  not as an uncontrolled `the only way` slogan.
- The MN 10 opening line and the repeated practical qualifier line are now
  explicit phrase records in `terms/` rather than document-only choices.

## Re-audit Calls

- `so satova assasati satova passasati` is aligned with the current MN 118
  handling: `One breathes in remembering the Dhamma; one breathes out
  remembering the Dhamma.` That shared breathing-side line now has its own
  governed phrase record, so MN 10 and MN 118 can stay synchronized by live
  policy rather than by document-local memory.
- `ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṁ` is rendered
  `ardent, with clear knowing, with remembering, having removed coveting and
  distress regarding the world.` The line keeps `sati` and `sampajañña`
  distinct and does not slide back into mindfulness-jargon.
- The internal / external refrain is kept source-facing as `internal`,
  `external`, and `both internal and external`, not re-explained as subjective
  versus objective or as self versus other.
- `'Atthi kāyo'ti ... anissito ca viharati, na ca kiñci loke upādiyati` is
  handled as a practical anchor: `'There is body,' just enough for knowing and
  for remembering ... does not take anything in the world personally.` It is
  not treated as a metaphysical existence statement.
- The citta-state list now uses `contracted / scattered`, `expanded /
  unexpanded`, `surpassable / unsurpassable`, `composed / uncomposed`, and
  `released / unreleased`. Those choices fit the current house voice, but they
  are still more translation-local than the stronger cluster-governed families.
- `dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati` remains `one remains observing dhammas
  among dhammas`, in line with the current `dhamma` record.
- `upādāna` force in the refrain ending is carried through as `does not take
  anything in the world personally`, not generic non-attachment rhetoric.

## Practice Clarifications

- This edition does not treat MN 10 as a discourse about bare attention,
  passive noting, or technique collection.
- It is handled as a structured training in remembering and clear knowing
  across body, felt experience, feeling mind, and dhammas.
- The body branch is not isolated from MN 118. The breathing subsection is
  translated so the family relation remains visible.
- The dhamma branch is not reduced to doctrinal study. The discourse keeps the
  hindrances, clung-to heaps, sense fields, awakening factors, and noble truths
  inside active observation and discernment.
- The closing fruit formula is kept plain and practical rather than inflated
  into attainment rhetoric.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the discourse can function
  as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in
  the root text itself.
- The first full refrain in each major branch is laid out in full.
- Later formally identical refrains are compacted with bracketed refrain cues
  once the governing pattern is fully clear.
- The charnel-ground sequence is deliberately compacted into a readable staged
  list rather than repeated line by line.

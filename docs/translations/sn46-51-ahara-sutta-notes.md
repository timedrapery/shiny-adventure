# SN 46.51 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[SN 46.51: Ahara Sutta](sn46-51-ahara-sutta.md). The main translation is the
primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of SN 46.51 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially `ahara`,
  `nivarana`, `kamacchanda`, `byapada`, `thina-middha`,
  `uddhacca-kukkucca`, `vicikiccha`, `yoniso-manasikara`,
  `ayoniso-manasikara`, `manasikara`, `bojjhanga`, `sati`,
  `dhammavicaya`, `viriya`, `piti`, `passaddhi`, `samadhi`, `upekkha`,
  `metta`, and `metta-cetovimutti`.
- Working method: the discourse was governed as the next strategic
  translation-surface expansion after SN 36.6, with the repeated feeding and
  starving formulas treated as a control surface for the distraction and
  awakening-factor families.

## Governing Decisions

- `ahara` and `anahara` are rendered here with the active pair `feeds` and
  `starves`. The headword default for `ahara` remains `nutriment`, but this
  discourse works more naturally as an operational feeding / starving pattern
  than as repeated abstract nutriment language.
- The five `nivarana` members follow the repository's governed renderings:
  `sensual distraction`, `aversive distraction`, `dull distraction`,
  `agitated distraction`, and `uncertain distraction`.
- The seven `bojjhanga` members follow the repository's established framework
  renderings: `remembering awakening factor`, `discernment-of-qualities
  awakening factor`, `energy awakening factor`, `delight awakening factor`,
  `relaxation awakening factor`, `mental composure awakening factor`, and
  `dynamic-balance awakening factor`.
- `yonisomanasikarabahulikaro` and `ayonisomanasikarabahulikaro` remain
  `repeated wise attention` and `repeated unwise attention`, matching the
  headword example phrases already recorded in the live lexicon.
- The final block uses `amanasikarabahulikaro` -> `repeated non-attention`
  rather than rewriting the phrase into a second wise / unwise contrast that
  the Pali does not actually use.

## Existing Control Records Reused

SN 46.51 does not need new live phrase records to land as a stable translation
surface. The core repeated formulas already have enough governing coverage in
existing entries:

- `yonisomanasikarabahulikaro` -> `repeated wise attention`
- `ayonisomanasikarabahulikaro` -> `repeated unwise attention`
- `satisambojjhanga` and the other awakening-factor member compounds
- the fixed five-distractions family under `nivarana`

This pass therefore extends the translator-facing surface without reopening the
lexicon unless later translation work shows that a narrower formula record
would materially reduce drift.

## Re-audit Calls

- `subhanimitta` and `asubhanimitta` are rendered `the attractive sign` and
  `the unattractive sign`. This keeps `nimitta` aligned with the repository's
  default `sign` while avoiding later meditation-object jargon.
- `patighanimitta` is rendered `the sign of resistance`, keeping the local line
  coordinated with the repository's `patigha` handling rather than importing a
  generic aversion label too early.
- `arati tandi vijambhita bhattasammado cetaso ca linattam` is rendered as a
  practical list: `discontent, sluggishness, yawning, after-meal drowsiness,
  and the sinking of the feeling mind`. The point is the drift into dullness,
  not a polished literary cadence.
- `cetaso avupasamo` and `cetaso vupasamo` are rendered `the feeling mind's
  unrest` and `the settling of the feeling mind`. This keeps `citta` audible
  as the feeling mind while preserving the paired contrast.
- `vicikicchatthaniya dhamma` is rendered `qualities that support
  uncertainty`, and the matching bojjhanga formulas are rendered `qualities
  that support the ... awakening factor`. This keeps the repeated structure
  legible without pretending each support-list item has already been fully
  enumerated in the sutta.
- `arambhadhatu nikkamadhatu parakkamadhatu` is rendered `the energy of
  getting started, the energy of stepping forward, and the energy of pressing
  on`. This keeps the triple practical and progressive instead of collapsing
  it into one flat word such as effort.
- `samathanimittam abyagganimittam` is rendered `the calm sign and the sign of
  non-scatter`. The repo already has `samatha-nimitta` as `calm sign`; the
  second member is kept literal enough to avoid importing uncontrolled
  concentration idiom.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation breaks the discourse into four prose sections:
  `What Feeds The Distractions`, `What Feeds The Awakening Factors`,
  `What Starves The Distractions`, and `What Starves The Awakening Factors`.
- Those headings are editorial aids only. Their job is to keep a highly
  repetitive teaching discourse readable as a study document.
- Repetition is mostly preserved rather than collapsed, because SN 46.51 now
  serves as the repo's outward-facing feeding / starving control passage.

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first stable study edition of the repo's
  SN 46.51 translation surface.
- Future revisions should focus on the feeding / starving formula language, the
  `amanasikara` line, or any support-list phrase that later proves important
  enough to deserve its own governed record.

# SN 36.6 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[SN 36.6: Salla Sutta](sn36-6-salla-sutta.md). The main translation is the
primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of SN 36.6 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially `vedanā`,
  `sukha-vedanā`, `dukkha-vedanā`, `adukkhamasukha-vedanā`, `puthujjana`,
  `ariyasāvaka`, `paṭigha`, `anusaya`, `rāga`, `avijjā`, `nissaraṇa`,
  `soka`, `parideva`, `domanassa`, and `upāyāsa`.
- Working method: the discourse was governed as the next sensory-response
  surface after MN 137 and MN 148, with the one-arrow / two-arrows frame used
  to sharpen bodily pain versus added mental reaction.

## Governing Decisions

- `vedanā` remains `felt experience` at the headword level.
- The explicit three-register opening uses `pleasant feeling`, `painful
  feeling`, and `mixed feeling`.
- `assutavā puthujjano` remains `unlearned ordinary person`.
- `sutavā ariyasāvaka` remains `learned noble disciple`.
- `dukkhāya vedanāya phuṭṭho samāno ...` reuses the existing MN 148 painful
  feeling response lines:
  `when one is touched by painful feeling, one sorrows, grows worn down,
  laments, beats one's chest and cries, and falls into confusion`, and the
  inverse trained line.
- `paṭigha` remains `resistance`, and the latent compound remains `underlying
  tendency toward resistance`.
- `rāgānusaya` remains `underlying tendency toward passion`.
- `avijjānusaya` remains `underlying tendency toward ignorance`.
- `nissaraṇa` remains `escape`, including in the escape-from-painful-feeling
  line and the gratification / danger / escape sequence.

## New Control Records

SN 36.6 adds four live phrase records to the sensory-response surface:

- `sn36-6-two-feelings-painful-feeling`: `one feels two feelings: bodily and
  mental`
- `sn36-6-one-feeling-painful-feeling`: `one feels one feeling: bodily, not
  mental`
- `sn36-6-feelings-undiscerned-response`: `one does not discern those
  feelings' arising and vanishing, gratification, danger, and escape as they
  have come to be`
- `sn36-6-feelings-discerned-response`: `one discerns those feelings' arising
  and vanishing, gratification, danger, and escape as they have come to be`

These records keep the SN 36.6 surface auditable alongside the MN 137 / MN 148
sensory-response family rather than leaving its core contrast to this note file
alone.

## Re-audit Calls

- `kāyikañca, cetasikañca` is rendered `bodily and mental`. This keeps the
  second-arrow image tied to added mental reaction, not to the mere presence of
  bodily pain.
- `kāyikaṁ, na cetasikaṁ` is rendered `bodily, not mental`. This keeps the
  trained case precise: painful bodily feeling can still be present, but the
  added mental feeling is not.
- `kāmasukha` is rendered `sensual pleasure` in this passage because the line
  names the ordinary person's attempted escape from painful feeling through
  pleasurable sensuality.
- `saññutto` and `visaññutto` are rendered `bound` and `unbound` here. The
  wording stays local to the discourse's summary contrast and is not promoted
  as a new bondage-family headword default.
- The verses render the implied contrast as `added feeling` so the summary
  remains intelligible after the prose has established the one-arrow /
  two-arrows frame.
- `citta` in the verse is rendered `feeling mind`, keeping the affective center
  consistent with the repository's citta policy.
- `padaṁ ... virajaṁ asokaṁ` is rendered `the dustless, sorrowless state`. This
  is a local verse rendering, not a new default for `pada`.

## Practice Clarifications

- SN 36.6 is not saying a learned noble disciple avoids painful bodily feeling.
  It says the learned noble disciple does not add the second arrow of mental
  reaction.
- The ordinary person's second arrow includes sorrow, exhaustion, lamentation,
  chest-beating grief, and confusion.
- The ordinary pattern then turns toward sensual pleasure as the imagined
  escape from painful feeling, which leaves the passion tendency in place.
- The mixed-feeling line remains important: without discerning the arising,
  vanishing, gratification, danger, and escape in feelings, the ignorance
  tendency remains.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses prose headings for the contrast:
  `The Difference`, `Two Arrows`, `One Arrow`, and `Verses`.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in the
  root text itself.
- The repeated painful-feeling response is expanded rather than compressed
  because SN 36.6 is now a control passage for the bodily / mental reaction
  distinction.

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first stable study edition of the repo's
  SN 36.6 translation surface.
- Future revisions should focus on the one-arrow / two-arrows control records,
  the plural feeling-discernment lines, or the summary verse if stronger
  repository controls emerge.

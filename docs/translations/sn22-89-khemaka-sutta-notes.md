# SN 22.89 Translation Notes

This document is the companion rationale for
[SN 22.89: Khemaka Sutta](sn22-89-khemaka-sutta.md). The main translation is
the primary study surface; this file records debated translation choices,
source-audit calls, and edition notes that govern it.

## Source Base

- Primary source: the Pali text of SN 22.89 as segmented in SuttaCentral's
  Bilara data.
- Control surface: the repository's current term policy, especially
  `pañcupādānakkhandhā`, `rūpa`, `vedanā`, `saññā`, `saṅkhārā`, `viññāṇa`,
  `atta`, `attaniya`, `asmimāna`, `māna`, `chanda`, `anusaya`,
  `saṃyojana`, `orambhāgiya-saṃyojana`, `ariyasāvaka`, `arahant`,
  `āsava`, `anupādāya`, and the standard selfing and de-appropriation
  formulas.
- Working method: the discourse was governed as one argument about residual
  "I am" conceit in relation to the five clung-to heaps, not as a denial of the
  earlier SN 22.59 not-self surface.

## Governing Decisions

- `pañcupādānakkhandhā` is rendered `five clung-to heaps`, following the fixed
  collective expression rather than mechanically combining the headword
  defaults.
- The five constituent clung-to heaps are rendered with the same heap-family
  English as SN 22.59: form, felt experience, recognition,
  putting-together activities, and knowing.
- `attaṃ vā attaniyaṃ vā` is rendered `as self or as belonging to self`.
- `asmimāna` remains `conceit 'I am'`, with the exact quoted residue kept
  visible.
- `sakkāya-diṭṭhi` is not named directly in this discourse, but the translation
  keeps the distinction that the term records require: not regarding anything
  in the heaps as self or belonging to self does not by itself mean the subtler
  conceit "I am" has been uprooted.
- `māna`, `chanda`, and `anusaya` in the residue line are rendered `conceit`,
  `desire`, and `underlying tendency`.
- `orambhāgiya-saṃyojana` is rendered `lower fetter`, following the reviewed
  minor record.
- `udayabbayānupassī viharati` is rendered `remains observing arising and
  passing away`, keeping the practice ongoing rather than making it a single
  intellectual conclusion.
- `samugghātaṃ gacchati` is rendered `goes to uprooting`, matching the
  uprooting language already used for underlying tendencies in the live
  translation surface.
- The closing `anupādāya āsavehi cittāni vimucciṃsu` line follows SN 22.59:
  `were released from the outflows through not taking anything personally`.

## Re-audit Calls

- `āvuso` is rendered `friend`, matching the existing dialogue voice for
  peer-to-peer bhikkhu speech.
- The illness check is rendered in plain English: `it is bearable`, `getting
  by`, and painful felt experiences `receding` or `increasing`.
- `sandhāvanikā` is rendered `running back and forth`, because the line is
  about the messenger movement between Khemaka and the senior bhikkhus.
- The elders' inference about arahantship is left as an inference, not endorsed
  by the translation voice. Khemaka corrects it by distinguishing no
  heap-based self-regard from the remaining "I am" residue.
- `adhigataṃ` in Khemaka's reply is rendered `is found in me`. This preserves
  the presence of "I am" without over-stating it as an explicit view.
- The flower simile keeps the scent assigned to the flower as a whole, not to
  the petal, color, or pollen. This supports Khemaka's point that "I am" is not
  identified with one heap or with something apart from a heap.
- `anusahagata` is rendered `following along` and explained in notes as
  lingering residue. This keeps it close to the source image without replacing
  `anusaya` with a separate residue word.
- The cloth simile keeps `salt`, `lye`, and `cow dung` because the rough
  cleaning imagery matters. The remaining smell is not dirt exactly; it is a
  lingering trace after an earlier cleaning.
- The final sequence `ācikkhituṃ desetuṃ paññāpetuṃ paṭṭhapetuṃ vivarituṃ
  vibhajituṃ uttānīkātuṃ` is rendered with a direct explanatory sequence:
  `explain, teach, designate, establish, open, analyze, and make clear`.

## Practice Clarifications

- SN 22.89 is the key surface for keeping view, fetter, and underlying tendency
  apart in the identity family.
- The senior bhikkhus initially press a reasonable but incomplete inference:
  if Khemaka sees no self or belonging-to-self in the heaps, perhaps he is an
  arahant. Khemaka's reply shows why that inference is premature.
- The remaining "I am" is not a claim that form is self, that knowing is self,
  or that there is a self apart from the heaps. It is a subtler residue still
  found in relation to the five clung-to heaps.
- The flower simile blocks both reduction and separation. The scent is not the
  petal, color, or pollen, but neither is it meaningfully apart from the
  flower.
- The cloth simile explains how something can be clean and bright while a
  lingering trace remains. That is the discourse's practical bridge between
  abandoning lower fetters and uprooting the subtler conceit "I am."
- Observing the arising and passing away of the five clung-to heaps is the
  practice context in which the remaining conceit, desire, and underlying
  tendency "I am" go to uprooting.

## Editorial Presentation

- The main translation file uses section headings so the messenger exchange,
  Khemaka's correction, the two similes, and the final release result can
  function as a study text.
- Those headings are editorial aids, not claims that such headings stand in the
  root text itself.
- The source abbreviates several repeated heap lists. This edition expands
  enough of them to keep the argument readable while leaving some later
  summaries compact.
- The quoted "I am" language is intentionally repetitive because the discourse
  turns on a precise distinction between "I am" and "I am this."

## Edition Status

- This companion now supports the first stable study edition of the repo's
  SN 22.89 translation surface.
- Future revisions should be driven by source-audit gains, term-policy
  expansion, or real readability improvements, not routine wording churn.

## Translation

### The Setting

At Sﾄ」atthﾄｫ.

"Bhikkhus, I will teach the five heaps and the five clung-to heaps.

Listen to this.

### The Five Heaps

"And what, bhikkhus, are the five heaps?

Whatever form there is, past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse
or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near:

this is called the form heap.

Whatever felt experience there is, past, future, or present, internal or
external, coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near:

this is called the felt-experience heap.

Whatever recognition there is, past, future, or present, internal or external,
coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near:

this is called the recognition heap.

Whatever putting-together activities there are, past, future, or present,
internal or external, coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near:

this is called the putting-together-activities heap.

Whatever knowing there is, past, future, or present, internal or external,
coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near:

this is called the knowing heap.

These are called the five heaps.

### The Five Clung-To Heaps

"And what, bhikkhus, are the five clung-to heaps?

Whatever form there is, past, future, or present, internal or external, coarse
or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, with outflows and fit to be taken
personally:

this is called the form clung-to heap.

Whatever felt experience there is, past, future, or present, internal or
external, coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, with outflows
and fit to be taken personally:

this is called the felt-experience clung-to heap.

Whatever recognition there is, past, future, or present, internal or external,
coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, with outflows and fit to
be taken personally:

this is called the recognition clung-to heap.

Whatever putting-together activities there are, past, future, or present,
internal or external, coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, with
outflows and fit to be taken personally:

this is called the putting-together-activities clung-to heap.

Whatever knowing there is, past, future, or present, internal or external,
coarse or subtle, inferior or superior, far or near, with outflows and fit to
be taken personally:

this is called the knowing clung-to heap.

These are called the five clung-to heaps."

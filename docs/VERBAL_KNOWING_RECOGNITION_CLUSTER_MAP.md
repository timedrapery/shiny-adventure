# Verbal Knowing / Recognition Cluster Map

This document governs the repository's handling of the active verbal surface
around knowing, discerning, recognizing, and accomplishment-side knowledge:

- `jānāti`
- `pajānāti`
- `sañjānāti`
- `aññā`

It exists to stop drift across English verbs such as `knows`, `understands`,
`recognizes`, `realizes`, `is aware of`, and `comprehends`.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Main role | Do not collapse into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `jānāti` | `knows` | plain or baseline knowing verb | discernment, realization, liberation-talk |
| `pajānāti` | `discerns` | sharper practical knowing linked to discernment | bland understanding, completed full understanding |
| `sañjānāti` | `recognizes` | recognition / identification verb in the `saññā` family | generic perception, generic knowing, realization |
| `aññā` | `final knowledge` | accomplishment-side liberating knowledge | generic knowledge, vague realization, attainment-talk |

## Keep These Families Distinct

- `jānāti` is broader and plainer than `pajānāti`.
- `pajānāti` is the sharper verbal edge nearest to `paññā`, but it is still not
  `pariññā`.
- `sañjānāti` belongs with `saññā`: it recognizes or identifies, rather than
  discerning in the stronger `pajānāti` sense.
- `aññā` belongs near accomplishment-side liberating knowledge. It is not just
  another way of saying `ñāṇa`, `vijjā`, or `pariññā`.
- `sati` remains `remembering`, not knowing.
- `sampajañña` remains `clear knowing`, but as practice-side companion clarity,
  not as a replacement for the verb-family.
- `diṭṭhi` remains `view`, not knowledge.

## Verb / Noun Integration

- `jānāti` <-> `ñāṇa`
  Keep the broad knowing family coherent without inflating the verb into a more
  technical noun-level result.
- `pajānāti` <-> `paññā` / `pariññā`
  The verb belongs near discernment, but it is not yet completed full
  understanding.
- `sañjānāti` <-> `saññā`
  Keep recognition and designation surfaces coherent.
- `aññā` <-> `vijjā`
  Both can sit near liberating knowledge, but `aññā` is more accomplishment-side
  and should not replace `vijjā`.

## Formula Guardrails

- `yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti` -> keep the discerning force explicit.
- `nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānāti` -> keep the line plain and decisive without
  inflating it into enlightenment slogans.
- `aññāsi vata bho koṇḍañño` -> preserve accomplished knowledge rather than
  vague realization rhetoric.
- `vimutti-ñāṇadassana` remains noun-compound knowledge-language, not a reason
  to rotate the verb family ad hoc.

## OSF / Practical Guardrails

- Keep OSF and Dhammarato anti-mystification pressure explicit:
  these verbs should stay practical and source-facing.
- Preserve Punnaji's practical help where useful:
  distinguish ordinary knowing, clarified understanding, and liberating
  accomplishment without turning them into psychologized jargon.
- Preserve Hillside / Ñāṇamoli help where useful:
  context, restraint, recognition, and unobscured understanding can sharpen
  notes without replacing the governed defaults.
- Do not let explanatory notes replace the core defaults.

## Main Drift Risks

- `jānāti` -> deep insight or realization language
- `pajānāti` -> bland `understands`
- `sañjānāti` -> generic knows or generic perceives
- `aññā` -> vague realization or attainment-talk
- noun / verb mismatches across the same family

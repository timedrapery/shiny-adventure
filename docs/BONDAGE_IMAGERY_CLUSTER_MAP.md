# Bondage-Imagery Cluster Map

This note maps the repository's current handling of the bondage-imagery family.
It is not a general essay. It exists to keep translators from collapsing concrete doctrinal images into one vague English blur.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Functional Role | Do Not Collapse Into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `ogha` | `flood` | Overwhelming flood to be crossed | generic bondage, `āsava` |
| `yoga` | `yoke` | Yoking or harnessing force that keeps one tied in | `saṃyojana`, generic bondage |
| `gantha` | `knot` | Knotting or entangling force | `yoga`, `saṃyojana` |
| `saṃyojana` | `fetter` | Binding factor in awakening-stage analysis | every imagery term |
| `āsava` | `outflow` | Corruptive outflow that runs on | flood imagery, knot imagery |

## Operational Reading Order

1. If the text stresses being swept away or crossing over, prefer the `ogha` family.
2. If the text stresses being yoked, harnessed, or kept under a binding burden, prefer the `yoga` family.
3. If the text stresses knotting or entangling, prefer the `gantha` family.
4. Use `saṃyojana` when the text is in the fetter framework rather than metaphorical imagery.
5. Use `āsava` when the text is in the outflow framework rather than flood, yoke, or knot imagery.

## Family Logic

- `ogha` is flood imagery.
- `yoga` is yoke imagery.
- `gantha` is knot imagery.
- These are related bondage metaphors, but they are not interchangeable.
- `saṃyojana` and `āsava` remain separate rule-bearing families even when the doctrinal neighborhood overlaps.

## Translator Guidance

- Do not replace all three imagery terms with one safe English word such as `bondage`.
- Do not replace `yoga` with `fetter` by habit just because both are binding images.
- Do not replace `ogha` with `outflow` just because both use fluid imagery.
- Do not replace `gantha` with `attachment` or `bond` unless a local override is documented.
- Let the concrete image do real work when the text does so.

## Contrast Matrix

| If the text suggests... | Prefer | Avoid by Default |
| --- | --- | --- |
| being swept away, engulfed, crossed over | `flood` | `bondage`, `outflow` |
| being yoked, harnessed, kept under a load | `yoke` | `fetter`, `bondage` |
| being knotted, tied up, entangled | `knot` | `attachment`, `bondage` |
| canonical stage-of-awakening fetter analysis | `fetter` | imagery terms as substitutes |
| liberation by wearing away corruptive leakage | `outflow` | `flood`, `yoke`, `knot` |

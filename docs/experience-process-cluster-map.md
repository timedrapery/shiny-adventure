# Phassa / Vedanā / Saññā / Cetanā / Viññāṇa Control Map

This cluster governs one of the repo's highest-risk English drift zones.

These terms are not interchangeable with:

- `contact`
- `feeling`
- `perception`
- `intention`
- `consciousness`

unless a governed record explicitly allows those English words in a specific way.

## Core Distinctions

| Pali | Default | Functional role | Do not collapse into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `phassa` | `contact` | the contact event in the sense process | mere physical touch, vague encounter, sensation |
| `vedanā` | `felt experience` | immediate felt tone, pleasant/painful/mixed | emotion, mood, affect |
| `saññā` | `recognition` | marking and identifying | thought, concept, memory |
| `cetanā` | `intention` | directing / intending factor shaping action | desire, willpower, free will |
| `viññāṇa` | `knowing` | conditioned knowing tied to sense process | self, soul, pure awareness, generic sentience |

## Working Relation

- `phassa` is the contact event.
- `vedanā` is the felt experience conditioned by that contact.
- `saññā` is the recognizing or marking function.
- `cetanā` is the intending or directing factor.
- `viññāṇa` is conditioned knowing.

These can be closely related in one passage without becoming interchangeable.

## Key Drift Warnings

- Do not let `phassa` drift into bodily touch alone or vague encounter-language.
- Do not let `vedanā` drift into emotion or mood.
- Do not let `saññā` drift into thought, concept, or memory unless a narrower record explicitly governs that move.
- Do not let `cetanā` drift into desire-language or metaphysical will-language.
- Do not let `viññāṇa` drift into self, soul, witness, or awareness metaphysics.

## Formula Guardrails

- `phassapaccayā vedanā`
  Use `with contact as condition, felt experience`.
  Keep the pivot stable and do not collapse the two terms.

## Cluster Relations

- `phassa` belongs strongly with dependent-arising and sense-process analysis.
- `vedanā`, `saññā`, and `viññāṇa` also belong strongly with the five-heaps family.
- `cetanā` links strongly into kamma and nutriment analysis.

This means the repo should preserve one governed default across those clusters unless a narrower formula or compound explicitly overrides it.

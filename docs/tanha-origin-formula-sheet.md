# Taṇhā Origin Formula Sheet

## Purpose

This sheet controls the classic `SN 56.11` definition line for `taṇhā` so the
origin truth does not get re-solved locally.

Use it together with the live `taṇhā`, `samudaya-ariyasacca`,
`ya-tanha-ponobbhavika`, `nandirāgasahagatā`, `tatratatrābhinandinī`,
`kāmataṇhā`, `bhavataṇhā`, and `vibhavataṇhā` records.

## Control Line

- `yāyaṃ taṇhā ponobbhavikā` -> `that ignorant wanting that leads to renewed becoming`
- `nandirāgasahagatā` -> `accompanied by relishing and passion`
- `tatratatrābhinandinī` -> `delighting here and there`
- `seyyathidaṃ` -> `namely this:`
- `kāmataṇhā` -> `ignorant wanting for sensuality`
- `bhavataṇhā` -> `ignorant wanting for becoming`
- `vibhavataṇhā` -> `ignorant wanting for non-becoming`

## Formula Use

- Keep the line segmented rather than collapsing it into one loose paraphrase.
- Keep `taṇhā`, `nandī`, and `rāga` distinct.
- Keep `ponobbhavikā` in renewed-becoming language rather than forcing generic
  rebirth-causation wording.
- Keep `tatratatrābhinandinī` as recurrent delighting here and there, not as a
  neutral movement or an abstract attachment label.

## Drift Guards

- Do not translate `taṇhā` as generic `desire` in this formula.
- Do not collapse `nandī` and `rāga` into one English word such as `craving`,
  `desire`, or `pleasure`.
- Do not turn `ponobbhavikā` into `causing rebirth` unless a local frame
  explicitly requires that narrower wording.
- Do not flatten the threefold list into a generic list of wants; keep the
  compounds aligned with their governed headword records.

## Working Reminder

When this line appears in `SN 56.11` or parallel truth-definition contexts,
check it as a controlled formula before adjusting surrounding prose.

# First-Jhāna Entry Sheet

## Purpose

This sheet controls the standard handoff into the first mental theme so
translators do not re-solve the opening formula locally.

Use it together with the live `nīvaraṇa`, `pañca-nīvaraṇā`, `jhāna`,
`paṭhama-jhāna`, `vitakka`, `vicāra`, and `vivekaja-pīti-sukha` records.

## Control Sequence

- `so ime pañca nīvaraṇe pahāya` -> `having abandoned these five distractions`
- `vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi` -> `quite secluded from sensuality, secluded from unwholesome dhammas`
- `savitakkaṁ savicāraṁ` -> `with thinking and pondering`
- `vivekajaṁ pītisukhaṁ` -> `delight and ease born of seclusion`
- `paṭhamaṁ jhānaṁ` -> `the first mental theme`

## Use Rule

- Keep the hindrance-clearing lead-in visible before the first mental theme.
- Keep `kāmehi` in sensuality-language rather than narrowing it to sensual
  pleasures only.
- Keep `akusalehi dhammehi` as unwholesome dhammas rather than rotating among
  unrecorded moral registers.
- Keep `savitakkaṁ savicāraṁ` as `with thinking and pondering`.
- Keep `vivekaja-pīti-sukha` tied to seclusion and to the prior abandoning of
  distractions.

## Drift Guards

- Do not flatten the whole sequence into generic meditation-entry language.
- Do not let the hindrance line disappear when the first mental theme is
  translated in full.
- Do not turn `thinking and pondering` into scholastic jargon or trance
  rhetoric.
- Do not detach `delight and ease born of seclusion` from the hindrance-clearing
  frame that precedes it.

# Dhammarato Chanting Profile

## Purpose

This note records a source-backed liturgical term profile drawn from `The
Complete Book of Pali Chanting` by Phra Achan Dhammarato.

## Editorial Rule

When the chanting manual gives a rendering or strongly reinforces a liturgical
use that differs from current house style:

1. keep the current `preferred_translation` unless there is an explicit
   editorial decision to change it
2. add the chanting-book wording as an alternate, context rule, or note when
   it is stable and reusable
3. distinguish chant-critical liturgical vocabulary from one-off chant titles
   that do not need separate term entries

## Recurring Usage Tendencies

- chant headings strongly reinforce familiar liturgical renderings such as
  `recollection`, `homage`, `blessing`, `protection`, and `taking refuge`
- `puñña` language is often expressed as `merit` in pattidāna and dedication
  contexts
- `sīla` and `sikkhāpada` are repeatedly used in precept-request and
  observance formulas
- `uposatha`, `pāṭimokkha`, `pabbajjā`, `upasampadā`, `nissaya`, and `vassa`
  appear in formal procedural monastic settings
- `paritta` is treated as protective recitation and a stable chant-book genre
- `vandanā` and `anussati` language are central to morning and evening chanting
- `paṃsukūla`, `kaṭhina`, and related robe terms appear as stable ceremonial
  vocabulary rather than ad hoc translation choices

## New-Term Scope

This source justifies adding missing chant-critical headwords such as
`paritta`, `vandanā`, `anumodanā`, `pāṭimokkha`, `pabbajjā`, `upasampadā`,
`nissaya`, `paṃsukūla`, `kaṭhina`, `vassa`, `āpatti`, and `pārisuddhi` when
they are absent from the repository.

# Identity-Construction Cluster Map

This note maps the repository's current handling of the identity-construction family.
It is not a general essay. It exists to keep translators from collapsing related but non-identical terms into one English blur.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Functional Role | Do Not Collapse Into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `sakkāya` | `identity` | Appropriated identity built around the five clung-to heaps | `self`, `ego`, `personality` |
| `sakkāya-diṭṭhi` | `identity view` | View that takes the heaps as self or as belonging to self | `asmimāna`, generic `ego` |
| `diṭṭhi` | `view` | Held view or standpoint; not automatically blameworthy | blanket `wrong view` |
| `diṭṭhupādāna` | `taking views personally` | Appropriating views as something to hold and defend | plain `diṭṭhi`, mere `opinions` |
| `attavādupādāna` | `taking self-doctrine personally` | Appropriating self-theories specifically | generic self-concern, `asmimāna` |
| `māna` | `conceit` | Measuring oneself against others or standards | mere `pride` |
| `asmimāna` | `conceit 'I am'` | Residual self-conceiving; a sub-form of `māna` | `sakkāya-diṭṭhi`, `ego`, `identity` |
| `upādāna` | `taking personally` | General appropriative movement into `I` and `mine` | generic `attachment` |
| `upādānakkhandha` | `clung-to heap` | Singular heap under appropriation | mechanical recombination from headwords |
| `pañcupādānakkhandhā` | `five clung-to heaps` | Collective object of appropriation, self-view, and dissatisfaction | `five heaps`, `five aggregates of attachment` |

## Operational Reading Order

1. If the text names `pañcupādānakkhandhā`, translate the whole expression as `five clung-to heaps`.
2. If the text names `sakkāya`, read it through the five clung-to heaps and default to `identity`.
3. If the text names `diṭṭhi`, keep `view` unless a compound or context rule narrows it.
4. If `upādāna` is compounded with `diṭṭhi` or `attavāda`, preserve the appropriative force explicitly.
5. If the text names `māna` or `asmimāna`, do not drift into generic pride or ego language.

## Family Logic

- `diṭṭhi` is about view.
- `diṭṭhupādāna` is about appropriating views.
- `attavādupādāna` is about appropriating self-doctrine specifically.
- `sakkāya` is appropriated identity.
- `sakkāya-diṭṭhi` is the view built on that identity.
- `māna` is comparative conceit.
- `asmimāna` is the residual conceit `I am`.

## Translation Discipline

- Do not let `sakkāya` become the default English for every self-related context.
- Do not let `diṭṭhi` become automatically blameworthy English.
- Do not let `māna` become ordinary arrogance.
- Do not let `asmimāna` become pop-psych `ego`.
- Do not let `upādāna` compounds drift away from the stabilized clung-to-heaps construction.

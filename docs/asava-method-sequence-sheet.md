# Āsava Method Sequence Sheet

## Purpose

This sheet controls the `MN 2` seven-method sequence so translators do not
re-solve the outflow family locally.

Use it together with the live `āsava`, `āsavānaṃ khayā`, `āsavakkhaya`,
`manasikāra`, `yoniso manasikāra`, `ayoniso manasikāra`, `pahāna`, `saṃvara`,
and `bhāvanā` records.

## Cluster Defaults

- `āsava` -> `outflow`
- `āsavānaṃ khayā` -> `through the wearing away of the outflows`
- `āsavakkhaya` -> `the wearing away of the outflows`
- `yoniso manasikāra` -> `wise attention`
- `ayoniso manasikāra` -> `unwise attention`
- `pahāna` / `pahātabba` -> `abandoning` / `to be abandoned`

## Entry Contrast

- `ayoniso manasikāra` feeds the outflows: unarisen outflows arise, and arisen
  outflows grow.
- `yoniso manasikāra` blocks and abandons them: unarisen outflows do not
  arise, and arisen outflows are abandoned.
- Keep this as an attention contrast, not as a rationality-versus-irrationality
  slogan.

## Seven Methods

| Pāli line | Default rendering | Control note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `āsavā dassanā pahātabbā` | `outflows are to be abandoned by seeing` | Keep the seeing-side practical and clarifying; do not flatten it into vague knowledge-language. |
| `āsavā saṃvarā pahātabbā` | `outflows are to be abandoned by restraint` | Keep the guarding and gatekeeping force explicit; do not recast as suppression. |
| `āsavā paṭisevanā pahātabbā` | `outflows are to be abandoned by using` | This is disciplined use of requisites, not indulgence or consumer language. |
| `āsavā adhivāsanā pahātabbā` | `outflows are to be abandoned by enduring` | Keep the bearing-with force steady and practical rather than passive or resigned. |
| `āsavā parivajjanā pahātabbā` | `outflows are to be abandoned by avoiding` | This is intelligent steering clear of danger, not timidity. |
| `āsavā vinodanā pahātabbā` | `outflows are to be abandoned by dispelling` | Keep the active clearing-away force explicit; do not flatten it back into generic abandoning. |
| `āsavā bhāvanā pahātabbā` | `outflows are to be abandoned by developing` | Keep this tied to the development of awakening factors and allied qualities. |

## Drift Guards

- Do not replace the whole sequence with one vague English method.
- Do not swap `wise attention` and `unwise attention` for rational-application
  language unless a named source-comparison context explicitly requires it.
- Do not let `paṭisevanā` sound like indulgence.
- Do not let `adhivāsanā` collapse into suppression or mere passivity.
- Do not let `parivajjanā` sound like fearfulness.
- Do not let `vinodanā` disappear back into unmarked `abandoning`.
- Keep `āsavānaṃ khayā` and `āsavakkhaya` aligned with the `khaya` and `āsava`
  headword records rather than recasting them as generic ending or release.

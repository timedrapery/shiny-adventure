# Craving / Appropriation / Affective-Attachment Map

This cluster governs a high-risk stretch of English drift:
`desire`, `craving`, `attachment`, `passion`, `delight`, and `pleasure`
are not interchangeable in this repository.

## Core Distinctions

| Pali | Default | Functional role | Do not collapse into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `chanda` | `desire` | wanting, intention, directed interest; can be wholesome, neutral, or unwholesome | `taṇhā`, generic craving |
| `taṇhā` | `ignorant wanting` | thirsty, mistake-driven wanting; origin-side and dependent-arising wanting | generic `desire` |
| `rāga` | `passion` | heated affective coloring, attraction, stickiness | generic wanting, generic pleasure |
| `nandī` | `relishing` | savoring, delighting, affective relishing | `pīti`, `sukha`, generic happiness |
| `upādāna` | `taking personally` | appropriation, personalizing, taking as I or mine | generic attachment |

## Working Sequence

- `chanda` can be wholesome. It names desire or inclination without automatically implying ignorance or bondage.
- `taṇhā` is the thirstier, distorted wanting that drives dissatisfaction and conditions `upādāna`.
- `rāga` names passion or coloring rather than wanting as such.
- `nandī` names relishing or delighting in what is being wanted or savored.
- `upādāna` names appropriation: taking personally, holding as I or mine.

These terms can overlap in a live passage, but translators should not erase their distinct jobs.

## High-Risk Collapses

- Do not translate `chanda` as `craving` by default.
- Do not translate `taṇhā` as `desire` by default.
- Do not translate `upādāna` as `attachment` by default.
- Do not translate `rāga` as a loose synonym for wanting.
- Do not translate `nandī` as casual happiness, pleasure, or joy.

## Formula Guidance

- `vedanāpaccayā taṇhā`
  Use `with felt experience as condition, ignorant wanting`.
  Keep the line practical and immediate.
- `taṇhāpaccayā upādānaṃ`
  Use `with ignorant wanting as condition, taking personally`.
  Keep wanting and appropriation distinct.
- `yāyaṃ taṇhā ponobbhavikā ... nandirāgasahagatā ...`
  Keep `taṇhā`, `nandī`, and `rāga` distinct.
  Do not flatten the whole formula into one English noun such as `craving`.

## Wholesome Context Guardrail

The most common drift in this cluster is reading all desire-language as blameworthy.
This repository forbids that.

- `chanda` can be wholesome and may become `motivation` or `interest` in governed practice contexts.
- `taṇhā` remains unwholesome wanting.
- `nandī` is not equivalent to wholesome meditative `pīti`.

## Fixed Override Reminder

`upādāna` defaults to `taking personally`, but the heap-family compounds are fixed overrides:

- `upādānakkhandha` -> `clung-to heap`
- `pañcupādānakkhandhā` -> `five clung-to heaps`

Do not mechanically recompute those from the headword default.

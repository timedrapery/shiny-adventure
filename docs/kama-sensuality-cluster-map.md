# Kāma / Kāmacchanda Control Map

This cluster governs one of the repo's highest-risk English drift zones.

The terms in this zone are not interchangeable with:

- `desire`
- `pleasure`
- `sex`
- `lust`
- `enjoyment`
- `pleasant feeling`

## Core Distinctions

| Pali | Default | Functional role | Do not collapse into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `kāma` | `sensuality` | the sensual domain; sensuality and the pleasures sought through sense experience | `desire`, `pleasure`, `sex` |
| `kāmacchanda` | `sensual distraction` | desire for sensuality functioning as a hindrance | plain `chanda`, `interest`, `pleasant feeling` |
| `kāmataṇhā` | `ignorant wanting for sensuality` | taṇhā directed toward sensuality | generic `desire`, sexual appetite |
| `kāma-rāga` | `passion for sensuality` | rāga directed toward sensuality | generic wanting, pleasure, lust |
| `kāmupādāna` | `sensual clinging` | upādāna directed toward sensuality | attachment to pleasure |

## Headword Guardrail

`kāma` is governed as `sensuality`.

Use the controlled alternates:

- `sensual pleasures`
- `sense pleasures`

only when the passage clearly emphasizes enjoyable sensual objects or indulgence in them.

Do not default `kāma` to:

- `desire`
- `pleasure`
- `sex`
- `lust`

Sexualized English is reserved for narrower expressions that explicitly require it, such as `kāmesu-micchācāra`.

## Kāmacchanda Guardrail

`kāmacchanda` is not bare `desire`.
It is not wholesome motivation.
It is not mere attraction or preference.

It is governed as `sensual distraction` because the term sits inside the hindrance framework.

`sensual desire` remains a controlled alternate only when explanatory prose needs the lexical connection to `chanda`.

## Keep Separate From Nearby Families

- `chanda`
  Broader desire or interest; can be wholesome.
- `taṇhā`
  Ignorant wanting.
- `rāga`
  Passion or affective coloring.
- `nandī`
  Relishing or delighting.
- `vedanā`
  Felt experience or feeling tone.
- `sukha`
  Satisfaction or ease.
- `pīti`
  Delight or uplift.

Sensuality is not the same thing as pleasant feeling.
Sensual distraction is not the same thing as plain desire.

## Erotic-Register Rule

The repo now enforces this rule:

- bare `kāma` is usually broader than sex
- sexual English is justified only when a narrower record explicitly governs the line
- `kāmesu-micchācāra` is one such narrower record

Do not back-project the sexual register of `kāmesu-micchācāra` onto the broader `kāma` family.

## Sprint 1 Boundary

This control map stabilizes the headword and the nearest compounds needed for live translation:

- `kāma`
- `kāmacchanda`
- `kāmataṇhā`
- `kāma-rāga`
- `kāmupādāna`
- `kāmabhava`

It does not attempt a full normalization of every sensuality-related formula or every pleasure-related expression in the repo.

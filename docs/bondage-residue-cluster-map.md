# Bondage / Residue Cluster Map

This note maps the repository's current handling of the bondage and residue family.
It is not a general essay. It exists to keep translators from collapsing distinct doctrinal layers into one English blur.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Functional Role | Do Not Collapse Into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `kilesa` | `defilement` | Broad afflictive or defiling family | every narrower bondage term |
| `upakkilesa` | `corruption` | Narrower corruption or stain within the defilement field | every `kilesa`, every `nīvaraṇa` |
| `nīvaraṇa` | `distraction` | Actively obstructive state that covers over clarity in practice | generic problem-state, latent residue |
| `anusaya` | `underlying tendency` | Latent persistence lying underneath experience | habit, instinct, active hindrance |
| `saṃyojana` | `fetter` | Binding factor tying one to continued becoming | vague attachment |
| `āsava` | `outflow` | Deep corruptive outflow that keeps contamination running on | generic defilement, mere tendency |
| `pahāna` | `abandoning` | Putting away or giving up an unwholesome factor | final quenching by itself |
| `virāga` | `fading` | Cooling and fading of attraction or investment | numb detachment |
| `vossagga` | `relinquishment` | Release by letting go | suppression |

## Operational Reading Order

1. If the passage is practical and immediate, ask first whether the text is naming an active obstruction. If so, prefer the `nīvaraṇa` family.
2. If the text says the tendency lies underneath or remains latent, use the `anusaya` family rather than generic affliction language.
3. If the text is about what binds one to continued becoming or defines awakening stages, use the `saṃyojana` family.
4. If the text is about deep corruptive outflows and their wearing away, use the `āsava` family.
5. Use `kilesa` only when the broader defilement-family is really what the text is naming.

## Family Logic

- `kilesa` is broad defilement language.
- `upakkilesa` is a narrower corruption-family within that wider field.
- `nīvaraṇa` is active obstruction in practice.
- `anusaya` is latent persistence that can remain underneath.
- `saṃyojana` is binding force.
- `āsava` is corruptive outflow that keeps running on.
- `pahāna`, `virāga`, `vossagga`, and `nirodha` name different sides of removal, fading, relinquishment, and quenching. Do not merge them casually.

## Practice-Layer Guardrail

- The repository allows simple `ānāpānasati` explanation in which `mano` brings wholesome dhammas or wholesome thought-content to bear in order to gladden `citta`.
- That explanation may include countering `nīvaraṇāni` or other active obstructive states.
- It must not be overextended into an uncontrolled claim that a moment of gladdening has already uprooted `anusaya`, ended `saṃyojana`, or worn away `āsava`.

## Translation Discipline

- Do not let `kilesa` swallow every narrower bondage family.
- Do not let `nīvaraṇa` become a vague English for any mental difficulty.
- Do not let `anusaya` become psychology-lite habit-language.
- Do not let `saṃyojana` become generic attachment.
- Do not let `āsava` drift into generic defilement or archaic canker-language.

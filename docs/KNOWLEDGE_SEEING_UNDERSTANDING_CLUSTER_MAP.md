# Knowledge / Seeing / Understanding Cluster Map

This document governs the repository's handling of the main epistemic and
practice-adjacent terms:

- `ñāṇa`
- `vijjā`
- `paññā`
- `dassana`
- `abhiññā`
- `pariññā`

It exists to stop drift across English words such as `knowledge`, `wisdom`,
`understanding`, `insight`, `seeing`, and `awareness`.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Main role | Do not collapse into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `ñāṇa` | `knowledge` | broader knowing / knowledge language, often result-facing or formula-facing | generic `insight`, `gnosis`, mere information |
| `vijjā` | `clear knowledge` | liberating knowledge contrasted with `avijjā` | science, information, consciousness |
| `paññā` | `discernment` | cultivated capacity of discernment | generic wisdom-talk, intelligence, undifferentiated insight |
| `dassana` | `seeing` | seeing side of epistemic compounds and contemplative expressions | mystical vision, view, generic insight |
| `abhiññā` | `higher knowing` | elevated or directly realized knowing | occult power talk, vague advanced spirituality |
| `pariññā` | `full understanding` | completed understanding / doctrinal task-language | ordinary understanding, casual comprehension |

## Keep These Families Distinct

- `diṭṭhi` is `view`, not knowledge. A right or wrong view is still not the
  same thing as `ñāṇa`, `vijjā`, or `paññā`.
- `saññā` is `recognition`, not seeing or discernment.
- `sati` is `remembering`, not knowledge, not wisdom, and not bare awareness.
- `sampajañña` is `clear knowing`, but it is practice-side clarity paired with
  `sati`, not the same layer as `vijjā` or the broader `ñāṇa` family.
- `avijjā` is `ignorance`, explicitly contrasted with `vijjā`.

## Functional Roles

- `paññā` is best heard as a cultivated capacity.
- `ñāṇa` is broader knowing-language and often sits in formula or result
  expressions.
- `dassana` keeps the seeing side audible when the text uses both knowing and
  seeing.
- `abhiññā` marks raised or directly realized knowing.
- `pariññā` marks complete understanding where a doctrinal task has to be fully
  understood.
- `vijjā` marks clarified liberating knowledge rather than mere knowing.

## Formula Guardrails

- `ñāṇadassana` -> keep both sides explicit: `knowing and seeing`.
- `yathābhūta-ñāṇadassana` -> preserve conditioned-process precision; do not
  flatten into generic objectivity.
- `vimutti-ñāṇadassana` -> preserve the release-side knowing and seeing of the
  attainment; do not inflate it into mystical experience.

## OSF / Practical Guardrails

- Keep OSF and Dhammarato anti-mystification pressure explicit:
  knowledge-language should remain practical and release-facing.
- Preserve Punnaji's practical help where useful:
  `paññā` can be explained introspectively, `abhiññā` can be discussed in terms
  of directly realized or heightened knowing, and `pariññā` can be clarified as
  full comprehension.
- Do not let those practical explanations replace the repo defaults.

## Main Drift Risks

- `ñāṇa` -> `insight`
- `vijjā` -> information or abstract knowledge
- `paññā` -> generic wisdom-talk
- `dassana` -> mystical vision
- `abhiññā` -> occult sensationalism
- `pariññā` -> ordinary understanding
- `sati` / `sampajañña` -> generic awareness-language

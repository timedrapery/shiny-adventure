# Dukkha / Nirodha Scope

This note governs a core repository principle drawn from SN 22.86
(Anurādhasutta):

the Buddha says he makes known only dissatisfaction and the ending of
dissatisfaction.

In repo policy, this means:

- `dukkha` and `dukkha-nirodha` are not just one doctrinal topic among others
- they are a governing scope for how the repo organizes practice-facing and
  doctrinal language
- other stabilized clusters should remain readable within that frame rather than
  drifting into abstraction, metaphysics, spectacle, or self-help rhetoric

## Practical Consequence

This principle supports the existing OSF-facing handling that:

- one wakes up and looks at what is here
- sees where it is heading
- makes a wholesome change
- notices the quenching of dukkha
- repeats when remembered

It also supports the path-factor clarification that right view, right
remembering, and right effort are live functions inside that same frame.

## Anti-Drift Guardrails

- Do not let side-topics eclipse `dukkha / nirodha` as the repo's central
  practical axis.
- Do not let `nibbāna`, `suññatā`, `vimutti`, `ñāṇa`, or other major clusters be
  discussed as though they stand outside the ending of dukkha.
- Do not flatten this principle into a slogan; keep the doctrinal terms
  themselves governed by their own entries.

# Next Sutta Translation Roadmap

## Purpose

This note ranks the next recommended outward-facing sutta translation passes
for the repository.

The goal is not to choose the most famous texts in the abstract. The goal is
to choose texts that can reuse the repository's current governed clusters,
strengthen translator-facing surfaces, and reveal the next useful family-level
refinements.

## Recently Completed

- `SN 22.59` Anattalakkhaṇa Sutta is now live in
  [`docs/translations/`](translations/translation-documents.md).
- That pass now functions as the primary five-heaps / not-self control surface
  for the next round of translation additions.

## Sequencing Rule

Prefer suttas that:

- reuse already-governed doctrinal clusters
- pressure linked supporting terms rather than isolated one-off vocabulary
- produce reusable note-file rationale rather than translation-local wording
- improve the coverage of the current translation-surface maps and briefs

## Recommended Sequence

### 1. SN 12.2: Paṭiccasamuppāda-vibhaṅga Sutta

Why first:

- It is the strongest direct use of the current dependent-arising cluster and
  formula records.
- It has the deepest live-term support in the current repo: 68 term-source
  citations point to SN 12.2.
- It is the best short text for checking that `condition`, `quenching`, and
  chain-language remain coherent inside one governed translation surface.

Expected supporting-term pressure:

- Recheck the definition-side handling of `avijja`, `sankhara`, `nama-rupa`,
  `bhava`, and `jati` once they are read as one running explanatory sequence.
- Keep `jati` disciplined as `birth` unless the local frame clearly forces
  `rebirth`.
- Keep the four conditionality lines and the forward-link chain lines distinct
  from later explanatory glosses in the note file.

Likely note-file themes:

- `condition` versus `cause`
- `birth` versus `rebirth`
- the practical pivot from `contact` to `felt experience` to `ignorant
  wanting` to `taking personally`

### 2. SN 36.6: Sallatha Sutta

Why second:

- It is the best next expansion surface rather than a pure confirmation pass on
  already-translated structures.
- It extends the feeling-side work already visible in the MN 137 / MN 148
  sensory-response surface.
- It is likely to reveal the next valuable term-family refinement rather than
  only confirming current coverage.

Expected supporting-term pressure:

- It is the likeliest next translation to force a stronger decision on whether
  `assāda` and `ādīnava` need fuller governed treatment rather than remaining
  mostly formula-inherited.
- Recheck the distinction between bodily pain, mental distress, and wider
  reactive buildup.
- Decide how source-facing the arrow imagery should remain in the main text
  versus the note file.

Likely note-file themes:

- pain versus distress versus reactive proliferation
- feeling known early versus feeling doubled by reaction
- gratification, danger, and escape in the feeling family

### 3. SN 56.11: Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta

Why third:

- It is the strongest short public-facing four-truths translation still
  missing from the live outward-facing surface.
- It already has broad live-term support across `dukkha`, `nirodha`,
  `ariyasacca`, and the first-truth family.
- It adds a highly reusable discourse without demanding a major new doctrinal
  cluster buildout.

Expected supporting-term pressure:

- Recheck whether the first-truth summary line now deserves its own phrase
  record, as already noted in the MN 141 notes.
- Recheck the `dhammacakka` opening rhetoric so it stays forceful without
  slipping into inherited scriptural cadence.
- Keep the four-truths family aligned with the practical brief already used in
  the repo rather than letting the discourse drift toward slogan-language.

Likely note-file themes:

- the first truth as workable present-tense analysis rather than abstraction
- origin, quenching, and path held in one practical sequence
- the difference between public proclamation language and the later detailed
  analytical exposition in MN 141

## Reserve Queue

- `MN 9` Sammādiṭṭhi Sutta: best longer Sariputta analytical synthesis once
  the repo is ready for a broader coordinated pass across roots, dependent
  arising, and noble-disciple language
- `SN 22.89` Khemaka Sutta: best reserve choice if the next emphasis shifts
  from formula surfaces to the selfing / identity / residual-conceit family
- `SN 22.26` Aṭṭhena Sutta: best reserve choice if the next emphasis shifts to
  the five-heaps gratification / danger / escape triad after SN 22.59

## Operating Note

For each of these texts:

1. review the governing headwords, compounds, and formula records together
2. draft the main translation and the companion note file in one pass
3. add new phrase records only where repetition or drift risk justifies them
4. refresh any affected surface brief or map
5. run the full verification suite before treating the translation as stable

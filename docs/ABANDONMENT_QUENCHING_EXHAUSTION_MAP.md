# Abandonment / Quenching / Exhaustion Map

This note maps the repository's current handling of the abandonment and culmination cluster.
It is not a general essay. It exists to stop translators from flattening several distinct process and result terms into one English blur.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Functional Role | Do Not Collapse Into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `pariyuṭṭhāna` | `manifestation` | Active surfacing or taking-over of an unwholesome state | `anusaya`, generic obsession |
| `pahāna` | `abandoning` | Task of putting away an unwholesome factor | `nirodha`, `khaya` |
| `virāga` | `fading` | Cooling and fading of heat, attraction, and investment | mere `abandoning`, generic `cessation` |
| `vossagga` | `relinquishment` | Letting-go culmination or release by relinquishment | slogan-like `letting go`, plain `abandoning` |
| `nirodha` | `quenching` | Quenching or extinguishing | generic `cessation`, `khaya` |
| `khaya` | `wearing away` | Exhaustion or wearing out | `nirodha`, generic `release` |

## Process And Culmination

| Process-Side | Culmination-Side |
| --- | --- |
| `pariyuṭṭhāna` | `nirodha` |
| `pahāna` | `khaya` |
| `virāga` | `vossagga` |

This is a translator-facing aid, not a universal rigid sequence.
The texts can connect these terms closely without making them identical.

## Operational Reading Order

1. If the text names an unwholesome factor surfacing or taking over, prefer `pariyuṭṭhāna`.
2. If the text names the work of putting something away, prefer `pahāna`.
3. If the text names cooling, fading, or loss of charge, prefer `virāga`.
4. If the text names relinquishment or culmination in letting go, prefer `vossagga`.
5. If the text names quenching, extinguishing, or stopping at the quenching side, prefer `nirodha`.
6. If the text names running out, exhaustion, or wearing away, prefer `khaya`.

## Family Logic

- `pariyuṭṭhāna` belongs near active manifestation, not latent residue.
- `pahāna` is task-language.
- `virāga` is cooling/fading language.
- `vossagga` is relinquishment language.
- `nirodha` is quenching language.
- `khaya` is exhaustion or wearing-away language.

## Translator Discipline

- Do not translate all six with `cessation`.
- Do not translate all six with `abandonment`.
- Do not translate all six with `release`.
- Do not erase the existing house rule that `nirodha` defaults to `quenching`.
- Do not let `khaya` become just another way of saying `nirodha`.
- Do not let `virāga` become a stylistic synonym for `pahāna`.
- Do not let `vossagga` become a vague inspirational slogan.

## Formula Guardrails

- `asesavirāganirodha` should keep `fading` and `quenching` distinct.
- `āsavānaṃ khayā` and `āsavakkhaya` should keep `wearing away` distinct from `quenching`.
- `imassa nirodhā idaṃ nirujjhati` stays governed by the `nirodha` headword, not by `khaya`.

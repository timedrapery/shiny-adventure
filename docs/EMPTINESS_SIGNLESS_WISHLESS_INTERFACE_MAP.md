# Emptiness / Signless / Wishless Interface Map

This note governs the repository's handling of the
`suññatā / animitta / appaṇihita` interface.
It is not a general essay. It exists to stop emptiness-side contemplative
language from collapsing into one vague English blur.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Functional Role | Do Not Collapse Into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `suññatā` | `emptiness` | Emptiness-side designation, especially empty of self and what belongs to self | `nothingness`, generic `anattā`, mystical void |
| `animitta` | `signless` | Without taking up signs or markers | formlessness, blankness, trance-fog |
| `appaṇihita` | `wishless` | Without placing desire or projection toward an object or outcome | apathy, passivity, no-goals rhetoric |

## Family Coordination

This repository governs these three as a coordinated contemplative and
release-side family.

- They can appear together as three gateways or three interface modes.
- Their coordination does not make them interchangeable.
- Headwords govern compounds by default unless a narrower record documents an override.

## Relation To Nearby Terms

| Nearby term | Default | How It Differs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `anattā` | `not-self` | Predicate against self-identification; not the same as the emptiness designation |
| `nibbāna` | `nibbāna` | Governing consummation-side headword, not a replacement for the triad |
| `asaṅkhata` | `unconditioned` | Unconditioned designation, not a synonym for any one member of the triad |
| `vimutti` | `release` | Release-family term that the triad can interface with |
| `nissaraṇa` | `escape` | Way-out language rather than contemplative designation |
| `virāga` | `fading` | Process-side fading, not the same layer as wishless |
| `nirodha` | `quenching` | Quenching language, not a synonym for emptiness, signless, or wishless |

## Practical Function Without Mystical Drift

- `suññatā` governs practical emptiness of selfing and appropriation.
- `animitta` governs not fastening on signs.
- `appaṇihita` governs not placing desire or projection toward an outcome.
- None of the three should be used as premature bypass language detached from training.
- Practice-facing explanation may connect them with gladdening the mind,
  hindrance-clearing, composure, and release, but should not erase their distinct functions.

## Translator Guardrails

- Do not translate `suññatā` as `nothingness`.
- Do not translate `suññatā` as generic `non-self`; keep `anattā` distinct.
- Do not translate `animitta` as `formless`, `blank`, or `imageless` by default.
- Do not translate `appaṇihita` as `passive`, `apathetic`, `aimless`, or `no goals`.
- Do not let the whole triad become mystical poetry detached from doctrine and training.
- Do not use the triad as automatic substitutes for `nibbāna`, `vimutti`, `virāga`, or `nirodha`.

## Operational Reading Order

1. If the text highlights emptiness of self and what belongs to self, prefer `suññatā`.
2. If the text highlights not taking up signs, prefer `animitta`.
3. If the text highlights not placing desire or projection, prefer `appaṇihita`.
4. If the text names the family as a coordinated release-side set, preserve the three distinct English defaults.

## Support Layer

The family currently includes these governed compounds:

- `sunnata-vimokkha`

## Compact Contrast Matrix

| If the text suggests... | Prefer | Avoid by Default |
| --- | --- | --- |
| emptiness of selfing and ownership | `emptiness` | `nothingness`, `non-self` |
| not taking up signs | `signless` | `formless`, `blank` |
| not placing desire or projection | `wishless` | `passive`, `no goals` |
| family coordination as release gateways | preserve all three | flattening into one English label |

# Sense-Fields Cluster Map

This note governs the repository's handling of the `āyatana / saḷāyatana /
phassa` family.
It is not a general essay. It exists to stop field-language, contact-language,
and dependent-arising formulae from collapsing into organ-language or vague
experience-talk.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Default | Functional Role | Do Not Collapse Into |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `āyatana` | `field` | Governing family headword for the twelvefold field structure | base, domain, organ |
| `saḷāyatana` | `six fields of experience` | Sixfold experiential field structure in lived-process language | six sense bases, sense organs |
| `phassa` | `contact` | Earliest practical pivot where the family meets felt experience | sensation, feeling, encounter |

## Operational Reading Order

1. If the text names `āyatana`, keep `field` as the family default.
2. If the text names the sixfold structure, keep `saḷāyatana` as
   `six fields of experience`.
3. If the text moves into the contact link, keep `phassa` as `contact`.
4. If the text enumerates the internal and external twelvefold members, keep
   the specific `eye sense field`, `ear sense field`, `form sense field`, and
   parallel renderings coordinated across the family.

## Family Logic

- `āyatana` governs the twelvefold field map.
- `saḷāyatana` governs the sixfold experiential structure that functions in
  dependent-arising language.
- `phassa` is the contact-side pivot that arises with the field structure and
  leads into `vedanā`.
- The family should stay readable as lived process rather than as a list of
  physical sense organs or abstract metaphysical bases.

## Formula Guardrails

- `nāmarūpapaccayā saḷāyatanaṃ` ->
  `with name-and-form as condition, the six fields of experience`
- `saḷāyatanapaccayā phasso` ->
  `with the six fields of experience as condition, contact`
- `phassapaccayā vedanā` ->
  `with contact as condition, felt experience`

Keep those formula surfaces coordinated so the family remains intelligible as a
single process-layer.

## Translator Guidance

- Do not let `base` silently replace `field` across the family.
- Do not reduce the field family to physical organs alone.
- Do not let `contact` drift into feeling-language or generic sensation-talk.
- Keep the twelve-field map and the contact chain coordinated in the same
  English voice.

## Interface Guardrails

- Dependent-arising interface:
  the family sits inside the lived-process sequence and must remain readable at
  the chain level.
- Experience-process interface:
  `phassa` meets `vedanā`, but `contact` is not itself `felt experience`.
- Practice interface:
  sense-field language should remain concrete and usable, not scholastic jargon
  detached from lived process.

## Main Drift Risks

- `āyatana` -> base, organ
- `saḷāyatana` -> six sense bases, sense organs
- `phassa` -> sensation, feeling, encounter
- field compounds -> mechanically mixed `base` and `field` language inside one list

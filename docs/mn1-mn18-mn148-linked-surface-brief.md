# MN 1 / MN 18 / MN 148 Linked Surface Brief

## Purpose

This brief defines the current translator-facing linked surface across MN 1,
MN 18, and MN 148.

Use it when a passage moves through recognition, thinking, proliferation,
selfing, taking personally, identity, or practical de-appropriation.

The source of truth remains the live records in `terms/` and the translation
notes for the three discourses. This brief is a compact working map, not a
replacement for those sources.

## Surface Roles

- MN 1 is the control passage for `mannati` and the explicit selfing
  expansions around recognition, ownership, standing in, standing apart, and
  delight.
- MN 18 is the control passage for the sequence
  `phassa -> vedana -> sanna -> vitakka -> papanca` and for the
  `delight / affirm / take personally` line.
- MN 148 widens the same pressure across the six fields and adds the trained /
  untrained response lines, the arising and quenching of `sakkaya`, and the
  practical de-appropriation formula.

## Shared Defaults

- `phassa` -> `contact`
- `vedana` -> `felt experience`
- `sanna` / `sanjanati` -> `recognition` / `recognizes`
- `vitakka` -> `thinking`
- `papanca` -> `proliferation`
- `vinnana` -> `knowing`
- `tanha` -> `ignorant wanting`
- `mannati` -> `takes to be`
- `upadana` -> `taking personally`
- `sakkaya` -> `identity`

## How To Read The Three Texts Together

- Start with MN 18 when the passage needs the lived spread from contact
  through recognition, thinking, and proliferation.
- Read MN 1 when that same pressure becomes explicit selfing, mine-making, or
  standing in and apart from what is recognized.
- Read MN 148 when the six-field inventory, trained versus untrained feeling
  response, identity-taking, or release-side de-appropriation must be made
  explicit.

## Drift Guards

- Do not flatten `mannati` into generic thinking. MN 1 governs it as selfing.
- Do not detach `papanca` from the upstream MN 18 sequence of recognition and
  thinking.
- Do not let `upadana` collapse into generic attachment language. Keep the
  appropriative force explicit.
- Do not let `sakkaya` drift into vague self, personality, or ego language.
- Keep the `delight / affirm / take personally` family aligned between MN 18
  and MN 148.
- Read the MN 148 not-self formula as practical de-appropriation, not as a
  freestanding metaphysical slogan.
